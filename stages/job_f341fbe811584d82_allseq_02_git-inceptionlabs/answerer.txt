Here’s a straightforward, practical guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients (for one medium pizza)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈110 °F / 43 °C)
2 Tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Toppings (adjust to taste)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, olives, bell peppers, etc.
Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
1. Prepare the dough
Activate yeast – In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5‑10 min until foamy.
Mix dry ingredients – In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt.
Combine – Add the foamy yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead – Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 min, until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
First rise – Lightly oil a bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel, and let rise 1‑2 hours, or until doubled in size.
2. Make the sauce (while dough rises)
In a small saucepan, combine tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and optional sugar.
Simmer on low heat 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside to cool.
3. Assemble the pizza
Preheat oven – Set to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats.
Shape dough – Punch down the risen dough, then stretch or roll it on a floured surface to your desired thickness (≈¼‑inch). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pre‑heated stone.
Add sauce – Spread a thin, even layer of sauce, leaving a small border for the crust.
Add cheese & toppings – Sprinkle mozzarella, then arrange your chosen toppings.
4. Bake
Bake for 12‑15 min, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
For extra crust color, you can broil 1‑2 min at the end—watch closely.
5. Finish & serve
Remove pizza, let it rest 2‑3 min (helps the cheese set).
Garnish with fresh basil if desired, slice, and enjoy!

Quick tips for a great pizza

Hot oven = crispier crust.
Pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet improves bottom heat.
Don’t overload toppings – too many can make the crust soggy.
Optional shortcut: Use store‑bought dough or a pre‑made crust for a faster version.

Enjoy your homemade pizza!
