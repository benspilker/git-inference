Here’s a straightforward, step‑by‑step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

1. Gather Ingredients
Component	Typical Amount (for one 12‑inch pizza)
Dough	250 g (≈ 1 ¾ cups) all‑purpose flour, 160 ml warm water, ½ tsp instant yeast, ½ tsp sugar, ½ tsp salt, 1 tbsp olive oil
Sauce	½ cup canned crushed tomatoes, 1 clove garlic (minced), ½ tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp dried basil, pinch of salt & pepper, 1 tbsp olive oil
Cheese	150‑200 g shredded mozzarella (or a blend of mozzarella & provolone)
Toppings (optional)	Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, fresh basil, etc.
Finishing	Extra‑virgin olive oil, grated Parmesan, red‑pepper flakes (optional)
2. Prepare the Dough

Activate yeast (optional for instant yeast):

Mix warm water (≈ 38 °C/100 °F) with sugar and yeast. Let sit 5 min until foamy.

Combine dry ingredients:

In a bowl, whisk flour and salt.

Mix:

Make a well, add olive oil and the yeast mixture (or just water if using instant yeast directly).
Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.

Knead:

Transfer to a lightly floured surface. Knead 8‑10 min until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.

First rise:

Lightly oil a clean bowl, place dough inside, cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel.
Let rise 1‑1½ hrs at room temperature (or 30 min in a warm spot) until doubled in size.
3. Make the Sauce
Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat.
Add minced garlic; sauté 30 seconds until fragrant (don’t brown).
Stir in crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
Simmer 10‑12 min, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. Set aside to cool.
4. Pre‑heat the Oven & Prepare the Baking Surface
Stone or steel: Place a pizza stone or steel on the middle rack and pre‑heat the oven to 250 °C (475 °F) for at least 30 min.
If using a sheet pan: Lightly oil the pan and pre‑heat the oven to the same temperature.
5. Shape the Pizza
Punch down the risen dough and turn it onto a lightly floured surface.
Using your hands (or a rolling pin), stretch/flatten the dough into a 12‑inch circle, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.
Transfer the dough onto a pizza peel dusted with semolina or flour (or onto parchment if using a sheet pan).
6. Assemble
Spread a thin layer of sauce (≈ ¼ cup) evenly, leaving a ½‑inch border.
Sprinkle shredded mozzarella evenly over the sauce.
Add desired toppings, distributing them evenly.
Drizzle a little olive oil over the top (optional).
7. Bake
Slide the pizza onto the pre‑heated stone/steel (or place the sheet pan in the oven).
Bake 10‑12 minutes, or until the crust is golden‑brown, cheese is bubbling and slightly browned, and toppings are cooked.
8. Finish & Serve
Remove pizza from oven; let rest 2‑3 minutes.
Sprinkle fresh basil leaves, grated Parmesan, or red‑pepper flakes if desired.
Slice with a pizza cutter or sharp knife and serve hot.
Quick Tips for Better Pizza
High heat: The hotter the oven, the better the crust. If your oven tops out at 230 °C (450 °F), bake a bit longer and consider using a broiler for the last minute.
Cold dough: After the first rise, refrigerate the dough for 24 hrs (or up to 72 hrs) for improved flavor and easier handling.
Steam: A quick spray of water on the oven walls (or a small pan of water) creates steam that helps the crust expand.
Thin crust: Roll the dough thinner and use less sauce/cheese for a crispier base.
