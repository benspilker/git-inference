Here's a simple, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza (makes one 12-inch pie):

Ingredients (dough for 1 pizza):
1 cup (240ml) warm water (about 110°F/43°C)
2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
2½ cups (300g) all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
2 tbsp olive oil
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
Toppings (basic Margherita style):
½ cup pizza sauce or marinara
8–10 oz (225–280g) mozzarella cheese, shredded or sliced
Fresh basil leaves
Olive oil, salt, oregano (to taste)
Steps:
Make the dough: Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy. Mix in flour, salt, and olive oil. Knead 5–7 minutes until smooth. Place in oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1 hour (or until doubled).
Preheat oven: As high as it goes (ideally 475–550°F / 245–290°C). If you have a pizza stone or steel, put it in while preheating.
Shape: Punch down dough, roll/stretch into a 12-inch circle on floured surface or parchment. Transfer to a pan or peel.
Assemble: Spread sauce thinly, add cheese and toppings. Drizzle with a little olive oil.
Bake: 10–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly and browned in spots. Rotate halfway if needed.
Finish: Let cool 2–3 minutes, add fresh basil, slice, and serve.
Quick tips for better results:
Don’t overload with toppings (soggy crust).
Use semolina or cornmeal under the dough for easy sliding.
For crispier crust, bake on the lowest rack or use a preheated stone.
