Watering an orchid with an ice cube is controversial and depends on the orchid type and potting medium.

Phalaenopsis orchids (moth orchids): Research suggests placing a few ice cubes on the bark medium once a week can be safe. The slow melt reduces overwatering risk, and root temperatures remain above damaging levels (~51–56 °F). Make sure water doesn’t touch leaves or aerial roots, and empty excess water from decorative pots.

Other orchids: Ice cubes are not recommended. Cold water can shock roots, slow growth, and increase rot risk.

Best general practice: Room-temperature water poured slowly until it drains from the bottom, letting the pot dry slightly between waterings. Ice cubes are a convenience method for certain Phalaenopsis only; monitor root health and leaf condition to adjust.

So, yes for Phalaenopsis with proper precautions, no for most other orchids.
