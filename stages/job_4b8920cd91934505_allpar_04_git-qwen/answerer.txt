Short answer: Yes, for Phalaenopsis (moth) orchids, watering with ice cubes is generally safe and effective when done correctly.
The method:
Place 3 ice cubes on top of the potting media once a week 
www.bhg.com
.
Avoid direct contact with leaves or exposed aerial roots 
u.osu.edu
.
Empty any water that collects in the decorative pot to prevent root rot 
u.osu.edu
.
Why it works:
Research from Ohio State University and the University of Georgia found Phalaenopsis orchids watered with ice cubes showed equivalent flower longevity, leaf health, and root health compared to those watered with room-temperature water 
u.osu.edu
.
The slow melt helps water absorb into bark media more evenly and reduces the risk of overwatering 
www.bhg.com
.
Media temperature briefly drops to ~51–56°F during melting—well above the ~20°F threshold that would damage orchid roots 
u.osu.edu
.
Important caveats:
This method is primarily validated for Phalaenopsis orchids grown in bark media; other orchid types may have different needs 
www.gardeningknowhow.com
.
If your orchid shows wrinkled leaves or silvery (dry) roots, it may need slightly more water; adjust accordingly 
u.osu.edu
.
Many orchid experts still prefer traditional thorough watering (soaking the pot) followed by complete drainage, especially for non-Phalaenopsis varieties 
www.kew.org
www.aos.org
.
Bottom line: If you have a common moth orchid and find ice cubes help you remember to water consistently without overdoing it, it's a reasonable, research-backed shortcut. Just monitor your plant's response and adjust as needed.
