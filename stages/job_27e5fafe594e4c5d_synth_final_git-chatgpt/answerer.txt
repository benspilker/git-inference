Here’s a clear, step-by-step guide to make pizza at home:

1. Prepare the dough:

Mix 500 g flour, 2 tsp yeast, 2 tsp salt.

Add 300–350 ml warm water and 2 tbsp olive oil; stir until a rough dough forms.

Knead 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours at room temperature (or refrigerate overnight for more flavor).

Punch down, divide into 2–3 balls, rest 15–20 min.

2. Make the sauce:

Combine 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes, 1–2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp dried oregano, pinch of sugar (optional), salt, and pepper. No cooking needed.

3. Assemble the pizza:

Preheat oven to 475–550 °F (245–290 °C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet.

Stretch dough into thin rounds on a floured surface.

Spread sauce, add cheese and desired toppings.

4. Bake:

Bake 7–15 min until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Rest 2–3 min before slicing.

Tips:

For a crispier crust, use bread flour and/or ferment dough in the fridge 24–48 h.

Avoid overloading toppings to prevent soggy pizza.

This combines reliability with flexibility for home baking.
