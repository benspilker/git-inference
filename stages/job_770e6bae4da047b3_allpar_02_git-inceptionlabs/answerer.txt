Here’s a straightforward, practical recipe for a classic homemade pizza:

Ingredients (makes one medium pizza)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active‑dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella (or your favorite cheese)
Desired toppings (pepperoni, veggies, olives, etc.)
Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
Step‑by‑step

Activate yeast

In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Mix dry ingredients

In a large bowl combine flour and salt.

Form dough

Add the foamy yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.

Knead

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8–10 min until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.

First rise

Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise 1–2 hours (or until doubled in size).

Prepare sauce

While the dough rises, combine sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a saucepan. Simmer low 10–15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.

Preheat oven

Preheat to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven now to heat.

Shape dough

Punch down the risen dough, roll or stretch it on a floured surface to your desired thickness (≈¼‑inch). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pre‑heated pizza stone.

Assemble

Spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border. Sprinkle cheese, then add your chosen toppings.

Bake

Bake 12–15 min, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly. For extra browning, switch to broil for the last 1–2 min.

Finish

Remove from the oven, let cool 2–3 min, garnish with fresh basil if desired, slice, and enjoy.

Tips for a great crust

Ensure the oven is fully pre‑heated; a hot oven yields a crispier base.
Using a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet improves bottom‑crust texture.
Avoid over‑loading toppings to keep the crust from getting soggy.

Enjoy your pizza!
