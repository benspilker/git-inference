Here’s a straightforward, practical way to make a homemade pizza.

Dough (basic two-step)

Activate yeast: combine 1 cup warm water, 1 teaspoon sugar, and 2 teaspoons active dry yeast; wait 5 minutes until foamy.

Mix dough: in a large bowl, whisk 2 1/2 cups flour with 1 teaspoon salt. Add the yeast mixture and 1 tablespoon olive oil; stir until a shaggy dough forms.

Knead and rise: turn out onto a floured surface; knead 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1 hour until doubled.

Sauce and toppings

Sauce: simmer 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes with 2 cloves minced garlic, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, a pinch of salt, and a drizzle of olive oil for 15–20 minutes.

Toppings: keep it simple—mozzarella, olive oil, fresh basil, and any favorites (pepperoni, mushrooms, peppers, etc.).

Assemble and bake

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C) with a baking sheet or pizza stone inside.

Shape dough: punch down dough, split if making two smaller pizzas. Roll or stretch into a circle, about 1/4 inch thick.

Add sauce, cheese, and toppings.

Bake: slide onto the hot surface and bake 10–12 minutes, until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Tips

For a blistered, chewy crust, bake on a preheated stone or inverted baking sheet.

If you don’t have yeast, a quick crust can be made with self-rising flour or by using a no-yeast tortilla-style base.
