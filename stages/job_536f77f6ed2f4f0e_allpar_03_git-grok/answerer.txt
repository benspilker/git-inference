Yes, in 2026 several cryptocurrencies and protocols exist where AI (or AI-like computation) plays a direct role in the "mining" or consensus process.

Key Examples
Bittensor (TAO): Uses Proof-of-Intelligence (PoI) consensus. "Miners" (nodes) contribute machine learning models or AI computations to a decentralized marketplace. The network rewards valuable intelligence outputs rather than raw hash power. This functions as AI-driven mining for decentralized AI training and inference.⁠Cryptopotato
Qubic: Employs Useful Proof of Work (UPoW), where miners (often called AI miners or Computors) train artificial neural networks as the core computational task for securing the network and earning rewards. This turns mining energy into productive AI model training instead of useless hash calculations.⁠Docs.qubic
Related Concepts

Other projects use decentralized GPU networks for AI workloads (e.g., Render Network (RNDR) for rendering and generative AI, or io.net for GPU compute). These allow GPU owners to earn tokens by providing compute power for AI tasks, functioning similarly to mining but focused on useful AI work rather than traditional Proof-of-Work puzzles.⁠Coincub

Proof-of-Useful-Work (PoUW) systems in general are gaining traction. They direct mining hardware toward real tasks like AI/ML training, scientific computing, or optimization problems, making the process more efficient and valuable.⁠Cryptominerbros

Broader Context

Traditional cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Kaspa, Monero, or Ergo remain mineable with standard hardware (ASICs/GPUs/CPUs), and AI tools now optimize those operations for better efficiency, energy use, and profitability. However, the query focuses on forms where AI itself performs or drives the mining, and the above examples best match that.⁠Blog.tokenmetrics

Note: Many Bitcoin mining companies are pivoting their infrastructure toward hosting AI workloads for higher revenue, but that's not AI mining the crypto—it's repurposing hardware away from crypto mining.⁠Coindesk

For practical implementation, check project docs for Bittensor or Qubic on running nodes/miners; most require GPUs or specific setups and involve contributing compute to AI tasks. Always verify current network rules, as crypto evolves quickly.
