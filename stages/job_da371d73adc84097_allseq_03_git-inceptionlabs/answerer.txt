Here’s a quick, practical recipe for a classic homemade pizza (makes one medium‑size pizza).

Ingredients

Component	Amount
Dough	2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour, 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp sugar, 2 ¼ tsp (1 packet) active‑dry yeast, 1 cup warm water (≈110 °F / 43 °C), 2 Tbsp olive oil
Sauce	1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, 1 clove garlic (minced), 1 tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp salt, optional ½ tsp sugar
Toppings	1–2 cups shredded mozzarella, plus any of your favorites (pepperoni, veggies, olives, etc.)
Finishing	Fresh basil (optional)

Steps

Activate yeast – Dissolve yeast & sugar in warm water; let sit 5‑10 min until foamy.
Make dough – In a bowl combine flour & salt. Add yeast mixture & olive oil; stir to form a shaggy dough. Knead 8‑10 min on a floured surface until smooth and elastic. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and rise 1‑2 h (or until doubled).
Prepare sauce – Combine sauce ingredients in a small saucepan; simmer low 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
Preheat oven – 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, heat it now.
Shape dough – Punch down risen dough, roll or stretch to a 12‑inch circle (thinner for crispier crust). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or pre‑heated stone.
Assemble – Spread sauce evenly, leaving a small border. Sprinkle cheese and add toppings.
Bake – 12‑15 min, until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly. For extra browning, broil 1‑2 min at the end.
Serve – Let cool 2‑3 min, slice, garnish with fresh basil if desired, and enjoy.

Tips for a great crust

Preheat the oven thoroughly; a hot oven yields a crisp bottom.
Use a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet for better heat distribution.
Don’t overload toppings—keep them light to avoid a soggy crust.

Enjoy your homemade pizza!
