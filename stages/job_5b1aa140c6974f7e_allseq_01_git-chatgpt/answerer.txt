Here’s a clear, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients
Dough (1 medium pizza)

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic (minced)

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, veggies, mushrooms, olives, etc.

Fresh basil (optional)

Steps
1. Make the dough

Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Combine flour and salt in a bowl.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil. Mix into dough.

Knead 8–10 min until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours (until doubled).

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a pan.

Simmer on low for 10–15 minutes. Set aside.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough to desired thickness.

Add sauce, cheese, and toppings.

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles.

Optional: broil 1–2 minutes at the end.

5. Serve

Cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.

Practical tips

Preheat the oven fully—heat is key for good crust.

Don’t overload toppings or it gets soggy.

A preheated baking sheet or pizza stone improves texture.
