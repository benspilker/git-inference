Here’s a straightforward way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

1) Dough (simple version)

Ingredients:

2 ¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (not hot)

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tbsp olive oil

Steps:

Dissolve yeast in warm water, let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

Add flour, salt, and olive oil. Mix into a dough.

Knead for ~8–10 minutes until smooth.

Cover and let rise ~1 hour (should double in size).

2) Sauce (quick)

1 cup canned crushed tomatoes

1–2 cloves garlic (minced)

Salt, pepper, oregano, olive oil

Mix and simmer 10–15 minutes (or use it raw for a fresher taste).

3) Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll/stretch dough into a circle.

Add sauce (thin layer).

Add cheese (mozzarella works best).

Add toppings (pepperoni, veggies, etc.).

4) Bake

Bake for 10–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Tips

Preheat a baking sheet or pizza stone for a crispier crust.

Don’t overload toppings—it makes the pizza soggy.

Brush crust edges with olive oil for extra flavor.
