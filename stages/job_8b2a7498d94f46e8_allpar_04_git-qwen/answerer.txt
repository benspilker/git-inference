How to Make a Basic Pizza at Home
Quick Overview
Prep: 20 min + 1–2 hr rise | Cook: 10–15 min | Temp: 475–500°F (245–260°C)
Ingredients (for 1 large pizza)
Dough:
3½ cups (440g) bread flour or all-purpose
1½ tsp instant yeast
1½ tsp salt
1¼ cups (300ml) warm water (~110°F/43°C)
1 tbsp olive oil (optional, for tenderness)
Toppings:
¾ cup pizza sauce or crushed tomatoes
6–8 oz fresh mozzarella or low-moisture mozzarella, shredded
Desired toppings (pepperoni, veggies, herbs, etc.)
Cornmeal or flour for dusting
Steps
Make the dough
Mix flour, yeast, and salt. Add water and olive oil.
Knead 8–10 min until smooth and elastic (or 5 min in a stand mixer).
Place in oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.
Preheat
Place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet in the oven.
Preheat to highest setting (475–500°F / 245–260°C) for at least 30 min.
Shape
Punch down dough, rest 10 min.
On floured surface, stretch or roll into 12–14" round.
Transfer to parchment or cornmeal-dusted peel.
Assemble
Spread thin layer of sauce (leave ½" border).
Add cheese, then toppings (less is more—overloading causes sogginess).
Bake
Slide pizza onto preheated stone/sheet.
Bake 10–15 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbles.
Finish
Let rest 2–3 min before slicing.
Optional: drizzle with olive oil, add fresh basil or grated Parmesan.
Pro Tips
Crispier crust? Pre-bake the dough 3–4 min before adding toppings.
No stone? Use a preheated cast-iron skillet or heavy baking sheet.
