Here’s a straightforward, classic pizza recipe you can follow at home.

Ingredients (serves 1–2)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active‑dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈ 110 °F / 43 °C)
2 Tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Toppings (adjust to taste)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, olives, bell peppers, etc.
Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Directions

Activate yeast – In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5‑10 min until foamy.
Mix dry ingredients – In a large bowl combine flour and salt.
Form dough – Add the foamy yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead – Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 min, until smooth and elastic.
First rise – Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise 1‑2 hours, or until doubled in size.
Prepare sauce – While dough rises, combine sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a saucepan. Simmer low 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
Preheat oven – Heat oven to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven now.
Shape dough – Punch down risen dough, roll or stretch it to your desired thickness (≈ ¼‑inch). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or preheated stone.
Add toppings – Spread sauce evenly, leaving a small border. Sprinkle mozzarella, then add your chosen toppings.
Bake – Bake 12‑15 min, until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly. For extra browning, broil 1‑2 min at the end (watch closely).
Finish – Remove pizza, let rest 2‑3 min, garnish with fresh basil if desired, slice, and enjoy!

Tips

A hot oven gives a crispier crust; preheat the stone for at least 30 min.
Avoid over‑loading toppings to keep the crust from getting soggy.
For a quicker version, use store‑bought dough and skip the rise step. Bon appétit!
