Watering an orchid with an ice cube is not recommended. Orchids (especially the popular Phalaenopsis) prefer a gentle, room‑temperature soak that allows the roots to absorb moisture evenly. Ice‑cold water can shock the roots, cause temperature stress, and lead to rot.

Best practice

Use lukewarm water (about 20‑25 °C / 68‑77 °F).
Water thoroughly until water drains from the pot’s bottom holes.
Let excess water drain completely—avoid leaving the pot sitting in water.
Water about once a week (or when the growing medium feels dry about an inch down).

If you want a slow‑release method, you can place a small, damp cotton ball or a piece of sponge in the pot, but avoid ice cubes or any very cold water.
