Here’s a concise step-by-step guide to make pizza at home:

Prepare the dough:

Mix 1 cup warm water (105–110°F / 40–43°C) with 1 tsp yeast and 1 tsp sugar. Let proof 5 min if using active dry yeast.

Stir in 2 cups flour, 1 tsp salt, knead 6–8 min by hand until smooth.

Coat with oil, cover, and let rise 60–90 min until doubled.

Divide into 2 balls, rest 15–20 min before shaping.

Make the sauce:

Combine 14–15 oz crushed tomatoes, 1–2 minced garlic cloves (or 1 tsp garlic powder), 1–2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp dried basil, and optional ½ tsp sugar. Stir well; no cooking needed.

Assemble the pizza:

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Stretch dough into discs, place on a floured or oiled surface/pizza stone.

Spread sauce, add cheese and toppings.

Bake:

Cook 12–15 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbly.

Let rest 2–3 min before slicing.

Tips:

For a crispier crust, use bread flour or a 60/40 mix with whole-wheat, and target 65–70% hydration.

Adjust toppings sparingly to avoid soggy pizza.

This gives a practical, no-frills approach that works reliably at home.
