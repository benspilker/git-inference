Here’s a clear, step-by-step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients (for one medium pizza)

Dough:

2½ cups all-purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)
2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings (suggested):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, veggies, olives, mushrooms, etc.
Fresh basil for garnish (optional)
Instructions
Make the dough:
Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.
Mix flour and salt in a large bowl. Add yeast mixture + olive oil. Stir until dough forms, then knead on a floured surface 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.
Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.
Make the sauce:
Simmer tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar on low for 10–15 min. Set aside.
Assemble:
Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C) — use a pizza stone if you have one.
Punch down dough, roll out to desired thickness on a baking sheet or stone.
Spread sauce (leave a border), add cheese and toppings.
Bake:
12–15 min until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly. Broil 1–2 min for extra browning if desired.
Serve:
Cool a few minutes, slice, and enjoy.

Tips for best results:

Hot oven + pizza stone = crispier crust.
Don’t overload toppings (prevents sogginess).
Less sauce and cheese often yields better texture.
