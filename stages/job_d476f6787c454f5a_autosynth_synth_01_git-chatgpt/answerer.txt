To keep an orchid alive and in healthy bloom, here are the key practices based on several sources:

Light: Orchids need bright, indirect light. East- or west-facing windows are ideal. If the leaves turn dark green or limp, it's a sign the plant isn’t getting enough light; yellowing or scorched leaves indicate too much direct sunlight.

Watering: Water about once a week during the growing season, reducing slightly in the winter. The potting mix should be nearly dry between waterings. Never let the pot sit in water. Ensure the crown stays dry to prevent rot.

Temperature: Keep orchids in temperatures between 65–80°F (18–27°C) during the day, and slightly cooler at night. A 10°F drop at night can trigger blooming. Avoid placing them in drafts or near heat sources.

Humidity: Maintain humidity between 40–70%. Use a humidity tray, mist the plant, or employ a room humidifier if the air is dry.

Fertilizing: Feed orchids with a balanced fertilizer (e.g., 20-20-20 or 30-10-10) at half strength every 2–3 weeks during the growing season. Reduce feeding in the winter when growth slows.

Repotting: Use a well-draining orchid mix (such as bark, perlite, or sphagnum) and repot the orchid every 1–2 years or when the medium breaks down.

After Blooming Care: Trim the flower spike back to the base or just above a node after blooming fades, depending on the orchid type, to encourage a possible rebloom.

By adhering to these guidelines—especially monitoring light, temperature, and humidity—you can help your orchid thrive and continue blooming healthily.
