Tie-dye as a technique wasn't invented at a single point—it's a modern umbrella term for ancient resist-dyeing methods with deep global roots:
The oldest known form, bandhani, dates to ~4000 BCE in the Indus Valley Civilization (modern-day India/Pakistan) 
www.nationalgeographic.com
.
Early tie-dyed textiles have also been found in China and Peru from around the 5th century CE 
www.wordans.com
.
Japan's shibori technique emerged by the 8th century during the Nara period 
tiedyewholesaler.com
.
However, the term "tie-dye" itself was coined in the mid-1960s in the United States, when the technique became iconic within hippie counter-culture 
www.quora.com
.
So: the methods are ancient (4000+ years old), but the name and modern popularization are mid-1960s American.
