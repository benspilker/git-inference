Sure — here’s a straightforward, practical way to make a homemade pizza from start to finish.

Short answer

Make a simple dough, a quick tomato sauce, assemble with toppings, then bake hot and fast until crispy.

Dough you can trust

Ingredients: 2 cups (250 g) all-purpose flour, 1 cup (240 ml) warm water, 1 teaspoon active dry yeast, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 tablespoon olive oil.

Method:

Activate yeast: dissolve yeast in warm water with a pinch of sugar; let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

Mix: combine flour and salt in a bowl, add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a rough dough forms.

Knead: on a lightly floured surface, knead 6–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Rise (optional but nice): place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1 hour at room temp or overnight in the fridge for deeper flavor.

Shape: punch down, divide if making two smaller pizzas, and roll or stretch to about 1/4 inch thick.

Tomato sauce (quick)

Sauté 1 clove minced garlic in a teaspoon of olive oil for 30 seconds.

Add 1 can (14 oz / 400 g) crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce. Season with a pinch each of salt, sugar (optional), dried oregano, and a bit of black pepper.

Simmer 10–15 minutes to thicken. Adjust with salt to taste.

Cheese and toppings

Classic base: shredded mozzarella (about 1–2 cups, 100–200 g).

Optional extras: sliced mushrooms, pepperoni, olives, bell peppers, fresh basil, chili flakes, or any toppings you like.

Assemble

Preheat: oven as hot as it will go, ideally 475–500°F (245–260°C). If you have a pizza stone, put it in the oven to heat.

Stretch dough: on a floured surface, flatten and stretch dough into a round about 12 inches (30 cm) across. Leave a thicker edge for the crust.

Sauce and cheese: spread a thin layer of sauce, leaving a small border, then sprinkle cheese evenly.

Add toppings: keep toppings light so the crust cooks evenly.

Bake

If using a stone: slide the dough onto the hot stone or a preheated baking sheet. Bake 10–12 minutes, until the crust is crisp and cheese is bubbly and starting to brown.

If using a pan: lightly oil a rimmed baking sheet, press dough to fit, add toppings, and bake for 12–15 minutes until golden.

Finish and serve

After baking, drizzle with a little olive oil, scatter fresh basil if you like, and slice.

Tips to improve results

Steam control: a hot oven is key for a blistered, crisp crust.

For a lighter dough: use half bread flour and half all-purpose flour; increases chewiness.

Make-ahead: dough improves after resting overnight in the fridge.
