How to Make a Basic Homemade Pizza
Dough (makes 2 medium pizzas)
Ingredients: 500g bread flour, 325ml warm water, 7g instant yeast, 10g salt, 15ml olive oil
Method:
Mix flour, yeast, and salt. Add water and oil; stir until shaggy.
Knead 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.
Let rise covered at room temp 1–2 hours (or refrigerate 24–72h for better flavor).
Divide, shape into balls, rest 30 min before stretching.
Sauce (simple & effective)
Crush 400g canned San Marzano tomatoes by hand.
Stir in 1 minced garlic clove, 5ml salt, 15ml olive oil, optional pinch of oregano.
No cooking needed—raw sauce brightens under high heat.
Assembly & Baking
Preheat: Oven + pizza steel/stone at highest temp (260°C/500°F+) for 45+ min.
Stretch: Hand-stretch dough on floured surface to 10–12", leaving a thicker edge.
Top: Light layer of sauce → fresh mozzarella (torn) → minimal toppings (less is more).
Bake: Slide onto hot steel/stone. Bake 6–10 min until crust is charred in spots and cheese bubbles.
Finish: Drizzle with olive oil, add fresh basil, slice immediately.
Pro Tips
Don't overload toppings—excess moisture = soggy crust.
Use a peel dusted with semolina/cornmeal to launch pizza easily.
Broiler finish: If home oven won't get hot enough, finish under broiler for 30–60 sec for leopard-spotting.
No steel? Invert a heavy baking sheet and preheat it.
