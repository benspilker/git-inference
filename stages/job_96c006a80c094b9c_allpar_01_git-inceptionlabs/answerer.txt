Tie‑dye is not a modern invention—it’s a resist‑dyeing technique that has been practiced for centuries. The earliest documented examples come from ancient Asia:

Japan (Shibori) – resist‑dyeing methods similar to today’s tie‑dye appear in Japanese textile traditions as early as the 2nd century BCE
1
.
India (Bandhani) – the Indian “bandhani” or “tie‑and‑dye” technique is also recorded in historic texts from the 1st century CE
2
.

So, rather than a single invention date, tie‑dye evolved over time from these ancient practices that date back roughly 2,000 years.
