Here’s a straightforward, practical guide to make pizza at home with dough, sauce, bake temps, timing, and tips.

Dough (yields 2 medium pizzas)

Ingredients: 2 cups bread flour (or all-purpose), 1 cup warm water (105–110°F/40–43°C), 1 teaspoon instant or active dry yeast, 1 teaspoon sugar, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 tablespoon olive oil.

Method:

Combine water, yeast, and sugar; let sit 5 minutes if using active dry yeast to proof. If using instant yeast, you can mix directly.

Mix in flour and salt, then knead until smooth and elastic (about 6–8 minutes by hand, 4–6 with a mixer). Add a little flour if sticky; add a touch of water if dry.

Coat dough with a little oil, cover, and rise 60–90 minutes at room temp, or until doubled.

Divide into 2 balls, flatten into discs, and let rest 15–20 minutes before stretching.

Tips: For a crispier crust, use bread flour or a 60–40 mix with whole-wheat, and aim for a dough hydration around 65–70%.

Pizza Sauce (quick no-cook version)

Ingredients: 1 can (14–15 oz) crushed tomatoes or tomato puree, 1–2 cloves garlic (minced) or 1 teaspoon garlic powder, 1–2 tablespoons olive oil, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, ½ teaspoon dried basil, ½ teaspoon sugar (optional), salt and pepper to taste.

Method:

Blend or whisk together tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, oregano, basil, sugar, and seasonings until smooth.

Adjust with salt, pepper, or a touch more olive oil to taste.

Quick no-cook note: If you prefer, blend tomatoes with a small amount of olive oil and seasonings; spread thinly on dough if you’re avoiding pre-cooking.

Tips: Use a thin layer of sauce to prevent soggy crust; sauce can be stored in the fridge 3–5 days or frozen for later.

Bake temperature and timing

Home oven (conventional): Preheat to 475–500°F (245–260°C). Bake 10–12 minutes for a thin crust, 12–15 minutes for a standard crust, until edges are golden and cheese is bubbling.

Pizza stone or steel: Place in the cold oven and preheat with the stone/steel inside for 30–45 minutes, then slide pizza onto it for crisper bottom crust.

For very hot setups (optional): If you have a grill or a dedicated pizza oven that reaches 600–900°F, cooking times drop to 2–6 minutes depending on thickness.

Tips: If crust browns too quickly, reduce oven temperature by 25°F and bake a bit longer; if underdone, give it a few more minutes.

Topping assembly

Base: Stretch or roll dough to 12-inch (30 cm) circle; transfer to a floured peel or parchment on a baking surface.

Sauce: Spread a thin layer (1–2 tablespoons per 12-inch dough) to avoid soggy crust.

Cheese: Classic mozzarella (shredded or sliced) or a blend with provolone; sprinkle salt sparingly.

Extras: Pepperoni, vegetables, mushrooms, olives, fresh basil, and a light drizzle of olive oil after baking.

Practical tips

Hydration: Aim for a dough that’s tacky but not sticky; adjust flour by 1–2 tablespoons as needed.

Kneading: Proper gluten development yields a chewy crust; you should be able to poke dough and have it slowly spring back.

Resting: A 15–20 minute bench rest helps dough relax and stretch better.

Pre-bake test: For thick-crust pies, you can par-bake dough 4–5 minutes before adding sauce and toppings to prevent sogginess.

Storage: On dough: refrigerate for up to 3 days or freeze for longer. Sauce can be made in larger batches and frozen.

Tools: A pizza stone or steel, a wide rolling pin or your hands for shaping, and a heat-safe peel or inverted baking sheet to transfer.

Example quarter-by-quarter timing (two 12-inch pizzas)

0–15 minutes: Mix, knead, and first rise.

15–105 minutes: Divide, shape, and rest dough.

105–120 minutes: Preheat oven/stone to 475–500°F.

120–132 minutes: Assemble toppings on dough.

132–144 minutes: Bake until crust is golden and cheese is melted.
