To keep your orchid alive and in healthy bloom, focus on these six essentials (most home orchids are Phalaenopsis/moth orchids):
🌞 Light
Provide bright, indirect light (east or west-facing window ideal) 
www.rhs.org.uk
Avoid direct summer sun, which scorches leaves 
www.rhs.org.uk
Leaf color is your guide: light green = good; dark green = too little light; reddish edges = too much 
www.thespruce.com
💧 Watering
Water weekly during active growth; reduce slightly in winter 
www.rhs.org.uk
Let the medium dry almost completely between waterings—check by pot weight or finger test 
www.southernliving.com
Use tepid water; drain thoroughly—never let roots sit in water 
www.rhs.org.uk
Avoid getting water in the crown (center) to prevent rot 
www.marthastewart.com
🌡️ Temperature & Humidity
Keep daytime temps 65–80°F (18–27°C), nights 60–65°F (16–18°C) 
www.yourorchidquestions.com
A 10°F (5°C) drop at night can trigger blooming 
www.justaddiceorchids.com
Aim for 40–70% humidity; mist aerial roots or use a pebble tray in dry conditions 
www.repotme.com
🌱 Potting & Roots
Use only bark-based orchid mix—never regular potting soil 
www.rhs.org.uk
Repot every 2–3 years or when medium breaks down 
www.marthastewart.com
Choose a clear pot to monitor root health: healthy roots are plump and silvery-green when wet 
flowerdeliverybickley.co.uk
🧴 Fertilizing
Feed weekly with diluted (¼ strength) balanced orchid fertilizer (e.g., 20-20-20) during growth season 
herebutnot.com
Flush with plain water every 4th watering to prevent salt buildup 
www.rhs.org.uk
Reduce or pause feeding in winter 
www.thespruce.com
✂️ Encouraging Re-bloom
After flowers fade, cut the spike just above the 2nd node to potentially trigger a side bloom 
www.rhs.org.uk
If no rebloom occurs and the spike browns, cut it at the base 
www.rhs.org.uk
Ensure adequate light—insufficient light is the #1 reason orchids won't rebloom 
www.aos.org
⚠️ Quick troubleshooting
Wrinkled leaves? Likely underwatered or root rot—check roots 
www.aos.org
No flowers? Try a month in a slightly cooler spot (5°F/3°C drop) to stimulate blooming 
www.rhs.org.uk
Yellowing leaves? Could be overwatering, too much sun, or natural aging of older leaves
Most orchid failures come from overwatering or incorrect light. Start there, stay consistent, and your orchid can bloom for months—and rebloom for years. 🌸
