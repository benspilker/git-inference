Here’s a clear, practical step‑by‑step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients
Dough (makes 1 medium pizza)
2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈ 110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil
Sauce
1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
Toppings (suggested)
1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, olives, bell peppers, or any favorite toppings
Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
Instructions
1. Prepare the dough
Activate yeast: In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5‑10 minutes until foamy.
Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt.
Combine: Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
First rise: Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel, and let rise 1‑2 hours, or until doubled in size.
2. Make the sauce
In a saucepan, combine tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar (if using).
Simmer on low heat 10‑15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
3. Assemble the pizza
Preheat oven: Set to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.
Shape dough: Punch down the risen dough and roll or stretch it to your desired thickness (≈ ¼‑inch). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pre‑heated stone.
Add sauce: Spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border for the crust.
Add cheese & toppings: Sprinkle mozzarella, then arrange your chosen toppings.
4. Bake
Bake for 12‑15 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
For extra browning, you can broil for 1‑2 minutes at the end (watch closely).
5. Serve
Let the pizza rest 2‑3 minutes, slice, and enjoy. Garnish with fresh basil if desired.
Quick Tips
Hot oven = crisp crust: Pre‑heat the stone or baking sheet thoroughly.
Don’t overload toppings: Too many can make the crust soggy.
Optional shortcut: For a faster version, use store‑bought pizza dough or a pre‑made crust and skip the long rise.

Enjoy your homemade pizza!
