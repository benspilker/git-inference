Here’s a practical, step-by-step way to make a pizza from scratch:

Ingredients (for 2 medium pizzas)

Dough:

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

2 ¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (≈110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Topping:

½–¾ cup tomato sauce or pizza sauce

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

Toppings of your choice: pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, etc.

Optional: dried oregano, chili flakes, or fresh basil

Instructions
1. Make the dough

In a small bowl, mix warm water + sugar + yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until frothy.

In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil to flour. Mix until a dough forms.

Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled in size.

2. Preheat oven

Preheat to 475°F (245°C).

If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat.

3. Shape the dough

Punch down the dough and divide into 2 balls.

Roll or stretch each ball into a 10–12 inch circle.

Place on a pizza peel (if using a stone) or baking sheet.

4. Add sauce and toppings

Spread tomato sauce evenly, leaving a ½-inch edge for the crust.

Sprinkle cheese and add your toppings.

Optional: drizzle olive oil or sprinkle herbs.

5. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Let cool 2–3 minutes before slicing.
