Here’s a simple way to make a homemade pizza.

Ingredients

For 1 medium pizza:

2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour.

1 tsp salt.

1 tsp sugar.

1 packet active dry yeast.

1 cup warm water.

2 tbsp olive oil.

For the sauce:

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes.

1 clove garlic, minced.

1 tsp dried oregano.

1/2 tsp salt.

1/2 tsp sugar, optional.

Toppings:

1 to 2 cups shredded mozzarella.

Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or anything you like.

Dough

Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.

In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.

Add the yeast mixture and olive oil.

Stir until a dough forms, then knead on a floured surface for 8 to 10 minutes.

Put the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it, and let it rise for 1 to 2 hours until doubled in size.

Sauce

Combine tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a small pan.

Simmer on low for 10 to 15 minutes.

Let it cool slightly.

Assemble and bake

Heat your oven to 475°F.

If you have a pizza stone or baking sheet, preheat it in the oven.

Punch down the dough and stretch or roll it into a round shape.

Put it on parchment paper or a baking sheet.

Spread sauce over the dough, leaving a small border.

Add cheese and toppings.

Bake for 12 to 15 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

Helpful tips

Preheating the oven well makes the crust better.

Don’t overload the pizza with toppings.

A pizza stone or hot baking sheet helps the bottom crisp up.
