How to Make a Classic Margherita Pizza

Gather Ingredients

Dough: 500 g (≈ 4 cups) bread‑flour, 300 ml warm water, 2 tsp instant yeast, 1 tsp sugar, 2 tsp salt, 2 tbsp olive oil.
Sauce: 1 can (400 g) crushed San Marzano tomatoes, 1 clove garlic (minced), 1 tbsp olive oil, pinch of salt, a few basil leaves, optional pinch of oregano.
Toppings: Fresh mozzarella (sliced or torn), fresh basil leaves, extra‑virgin olive oil, sea‑salt, freshly ground black pepper.
Optional: Crushed red pepper, grated Parmesan, sliced prosciutto, roasted vegetables.

Prepare the Dough

In a bowl combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt.
Add warm water and olive oil; stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead on a lightly floured surface 8‑10 min (or use a stand‑mixer with a dough hook 5‑6 min) until smooth and elastic.
Form a ball, coat lightly with oil, cover, and let rise at room temperature 1‑2 h or until doubled. For best flavor, refrigerate 24 h (cold ferment) then bring to room temp before shaping.

Make the Sauce

Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat, add garlic and sauté 30 s.
Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and torn basil; simmer 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Adjust seasoning; set aside to cool.

Preheat the Oven

Place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet on the middle rack.
Preheat to 250 °C (482 °F) (or highest setting) for at least 45 min. A hot surface gives a crisp crust.

Shape the Pizza

Punch down the risen dough and divide into 2‑3 portions (for 12‑inch pizzas).
On a well‑floured surface, stretch each portion with hands or a rolling pin into a 10‑12 inch round, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.
Transfer to a parchment sheet or a well‑floured pizza peel.

Add Toppings

Spread a thin layer (≈ ½ cup) of tomato sauce, leaving the edge clear.
Distribute mozzarella evenly.
Drizzle a little olive oil, sprinkle salt & pepper.

Bake

Slide the pizza onto the preheated stone/baking sheet.
Bake 8‑12 min, rotating once, until the crust is golden‑brown, cheese is bubbling, and the edges are crisp.

Finish & Serve

Remove pizza, drizzle a tiny splash of olive oil, add fresh basil leaves, and slice.
Let it rest 1‑2 min before eating to let the cheese set slightly.

Tips for a Better Pizza

Tip	Why it Helps
Cold‑fer the dough	Develops deeper flavor and improves texture.
Use a pizza stone	Retains heat, mimicking a brick oven.
Don’t overload toppings	Prevents soggy crust; thin sauce & cheese give a crisp base.
High‑heat oven	Quick bake creates a puffy, airy crust with a crisp exterior.
Finish with fresh basil & oil	Adds aroma and a bright, fresh finish.
