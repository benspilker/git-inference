Here’s a clear, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients

Dough (1 medium pizza):

2½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic (minced)

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings (examples):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, mushrooms, peppers, olives, etc.

Fresh basil (optional)

Steps
1. Make the dough

Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

Combine flour and salt in a bowl.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil. Mix into a dough.

Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours (until doubled).

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a pan.

Simmer 10–15 minutes on low. Set aside.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough to desired thickness.

Spread sauce, add cheese and toppings.

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Optional: broil 1–2 minutes at the end.

5. Serve

Let cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.
