Simple homemade pizza (makes one 12-inch pizza):

Quick ingredients:

Dough:

1 ½ cups (190g) all-purpose flour
1 tsp instant yeast
½ tsp salt
½ cup (120ml) warm water
1 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

½ cup canned crushed tomatoes or pizza sauce
1 garlic clove (minced), salt, oregano, pinch of sugar

Toppings (classic Margherita style):

1–1½ cups shredded mozzarella
Fresh basil leaves (after baking)
Olive oil, optional extras like pepperoni, veggies, etc.
Steps:
Make dough: Mix flour, yeast, and salt. Add warm water + oil. Stir until it forms a shaggy ball. Knead 5–7 minutes until smooth. Let rest 20–30 minutes (or use store-bought dough to skip this).
Prep sauce: Mix tomatoes/sauce with garlic, salt, oregano.
Preheat oven as hot as it goes (ideally 475–500°F / 245–260°C). Use a pizza stone or baking sheet (preheat the sheet too).
Shape: Stretch dough on floured surface into a circle. Transfer to parchment or cornmeal-dusted peel/sheet.
Assemble: Spread thin layer of sauce, add cheese and toppings. Drizzle lightly with oil.
Bake: 10–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles. Rotate halfway if needed.
Finish: Let cool 2 minutes, add fresh basil, slice.

Tips for better results:

Less sauce = crispier crust.
Room-temp dough stretches easier.
High heat is key for good char and chew.
