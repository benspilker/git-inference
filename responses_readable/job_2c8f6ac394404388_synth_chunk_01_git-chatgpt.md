Here’s a concise, step-by-step synthesis for making pizza at home:

1. Prepare the dough

Mix 500 g bread flour (or Tipo 00/all-purpose), 2–2.5 tsp yeast, 1–2 tsp salt.

Add 300–338 ml lukewarm water and 1 tbsp olive oil; stir until shaggy.

Knead 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.

Let rise in a lightly oiled bowl, covered, 1–2 h at room temperature until doubled.

Punch down, divide into balls, rest 15–20 min before shaping.

2. Make the sauce

Combine 1 can (≈400 g) crushed tomatoes, 1–3 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp dried oregano, ½ tsp salt, pinch of sugar; optionally add olive oil, balsamic, or fresh basil.

Mix well; no cooking required for a fresh-tasting sauce.

3. Assemble & bake

Preheat oven as hot as possible (≈475–500 °F / 245–260 °C); use a pizza stone or heavy tray if available.

Stretch dough into rounds by hand, spread a thin layer of sauce, add desired toppings.

Bake 10–15 min until crust is golden and toppings are cooked.

Optional: Refrigerate dough 24–48 h for deeper flavor and crisper crust.

This blends a practical home approach with flexibility for flavor and texture.
