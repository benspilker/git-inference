Here’s a clear, step‑by‑step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients
Dough (makes 1 medium pizza)
2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈ 110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil
Sauce
1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
Toppings (suggested)
1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or any favorite toppings
Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
Instructions

Prepare the dough

In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.
Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead on a lightly floured surface for 8–10 min, until smooth and elastic.
Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise 1–2 hours, until doubled in size.

Make the sauce

Combine the tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and optional sugar in a small saucepan.
Simmer on low heat for 10–15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.

Preheat the oven

Preheat to 475 °F (245 °C).
If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats.

Shape the crust

Punch down the risen dough and roll or stretch it to your desired thickness (≈ ¼‑inch for a medium crust).
Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a preheated pizza stone.

Assemble the pizza

Spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border around the edges.
Sprinkle shredded mozzarella, then add your chosen toppings.

Bake

Bake for 12–15 min, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
For extra browning, you can broil for 1–2 min at the end (watch closely).

Finish & serve

Remove from the oven, let cool 2–3 min, then slice.
Garnish with fresh basil if desired, and enjoy!
Tips for a Great Pizza
Hot oven: A fully preheated oven (or stone) gives a crispier crust.
Stone or inverted sheet: Improves bottom‑crust texture.
Don’t overload toppings: Too many can make the crust soggy.
Quick‑rise option: If short on time, let the dough rise for 30 min at room temperature and still get a decent crust.

Enjoy your homemade pizza!
