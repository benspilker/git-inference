Yes — but with an important distinction:

👉 There are higher-capacity discs than standard Blu-ray that you can burn at home
👉 And there are much higher-capacity optical formats — but you usually can’t burn them with consumer drives

🔵 1. Higher-capacity “burnable” Blu-ray (what you want)

These are called BDXL discs — basically an extension of Blu-ray.

Example options:
Sony 128GB BDXL BD‑R XL Disc · $44.99 · 
4.9
Quad-layer Blu‑ray disc with up to 128GB capacity for high-density data burning.
Verbatim 100GB BD‑R XL Blu‑ray Disc · $27.32
Triple-layer Blu‑ray disc offering 100GB storage on a single writable disc.
Capacity tiers:

Standard Blu-ray:

25GB (single layer)

50GB (dual layer)

BDXL (what you’re asking about):

100GB (triple layer)

128GB (quad layer)

✅ These work similarly to normal Blu-ray
❗ You need a BDXL-compatible burner (older Blu-ray drives won’t work)

🧱 2. “M-DISC” (same size, but more durable)

You’ll also see M-DISC BDXL versions:

Same capacity (100GB–128GB)

Designed for long-term archival (decades–centuries)

Example:

Verbatim M-DISC BDXL (100GB)

👉 Not higher capacity — just more durable

🧪 3. Much higher capacity optical discs (NOT consumer-burnable)

These go way beyond Blu-ray, but come with a catch:

Example:
Sony 5.5TB Optical Disc Archive Cartridge · $199.47
Professional multi-disc optical cartridge system with terabyte-scale storage capacity.

There are also:

300GB, 600GB, 1.5TB cartridges

Multi-disc systems inside one cartridge

⚠️ Downsides:

Require special enterprise hardware

Not compatible with normal Blu-ray drives

Used by studios, broadcasters, archives

⚖️ Quick comparison
Type	Max Capacity	Burn at Home?	Notes

BDXL	128GB	✅ (with BDXL drive)	Best consumer option
M-DISC BDXL	100–128GB	✅	Longer lifespan
Optical Disc Archive	300GB–5.5TB+	❌	Professional only
🧠 Bottom line

✅ Yes — BDXL discs (100GB–128GB) are the “bigger Blu-ray” you’re looking for

❌ Anything significantly larger (TB-scale) exists but isn’t consumer-burnable
