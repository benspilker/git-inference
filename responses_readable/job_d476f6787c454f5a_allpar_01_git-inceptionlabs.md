Keeping an orchid thriving and blooming regularly involves a few key habits:

Aspect	What to Do	Why It Matters
Light	Place the orchid where it gets bright, indirect sunlight (an east‑ or west‑facing window works well). If the leaves look dark green and limp, it’s not getting enough light; yellowing or scorched spots mean too much.	Orchids need strong, filtered light to produce flowers.
Water	Water about once a week in winter and every 5‑7 days in summer. Use room‑temperature water and let it drain completely; never let the pot sit in a saucer of water. A good rule: feel the potting mix—if the top 1‑2 cm is dry, it’s time to water.	Over‑watering causes root rot; under‑watering stalls growth.
Air‑flow & Humidity	Keep humidity around 40‑60 %. Use a pebble tray with water or a room humidifier, especially in dry indoor environments. Ensure gentle air circulation (a fan on low speed a a few feet away).	Proper humidity and airflow prevent fungal issues and help the plant “breathe.”
Fertilizer	Feed with a balanced (20‑20‑20) orchid fertilizer diluted to half strength, every 2‑3 weeks during the growing season (spring‑summer). Reduce feeding in fall/winter when growth slows.	Nutrients support leaf health and flower production.
Potting Media	Use a well‑draining mix such as bark, sphagnum moss, or a commercial orchid blend. Repot every 12‑18 months or when the media breaks down, typically after a blooming cycle.	Fresh media supplies oxygen to roots and prevents waterlogging.
Temperature	Aim for daytime temps of 65‑75 °F (18‑24 °C) and nighttime temps 55‑65 °F (13‑18 °C). A slight drop at night encourages blooming.	Temperature swings mimic the plant’s natural environment and trigger flower spikes.
Pruning	After flowers fade, cut the flower spike back to the node just below the bloom (or cut it off entirely if it’s brown). This encourages the plant to put energy into new growth rather than trying to rebloom on a weak stem.	Proper pruning redirects energy to healthy growth.
Pests	Inspect leaves and roots regularly for mealybugs, scale, or spider mites. Treat early with a gentle insecticidal soap or neem oil.	Early pest control prevents damage that can stop blooming.
Quick Checklist for a Healthy Bloom Cycle
Light – Bright, indirect.
Water – Once a week; let drain.
Humidity – 40‑60 %; use tray or humidifier.
Fertilize – Half‑strength, every 2‑3 weeks (spring‑summer).
Repot – Every 12‑18 months with fresh bark mix.
Temperature – Day 65‑75 °F, night 55‑65 °F.
Prune – Cut spent spikes after blooming.
Inspect – Weekly pest check.

Following these steps should keep your orchid vigorous and encourage repeat blooms throughout the year.
