Homemade Pizza at Home (Simple, Reliable Method)

1. Dough (makes 2 × 12–14" pizzas)

Ingredients (basic no-knead or quick version):

500 g (4 cups) bread flour or all-purpose flour
300–325 ml (1¼–1⅓ cups) lukewarm water
2 tsp instant yeast (or 7 g packet)
2 tsp salt
2 tbsp olive oil

Quick steps:

Mix dry ingredients (flour, yeast, salt).
Add water + olive oil. Stir until shaggy dough forms (no heavy kneading needed).
Cover and let rise 1–2 hours at room temp (or overnight in fridge for better flavor).
Divide into 2 balls, rest 15–20 min, then stretch by hand into thin rounds (don't use rolling pin if possible — it deflates air pockets).

Tip: For crispier crust, use bread flour and let dough cold-ferment in fridge 24–48 hrs.

2. Sauce (simple classic)
1 can (400 g / 14 oz) crushed tomatoes or passata
1–2 garlic cloves, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
Pinch of sugar + black pepper
Optional: fresh basil, red pepper flakes

Mix everything cold — no cooking needed for fresh flavor. Spread thinly (too much sauce makes soggy pizza).

3. Assembly & Toppings
Preheat oven as hot as it goes (ideally 475–550°F / 245–290°C). Use a pizza stone or steel if you have one; otherwise a heavy baking sheet flipped upside-down.
Stretch dough on floured parchment or semolina.
Add thin layer of sauce, then cheese (fresh mozzarella torn into pieces + low-moisture mozzarella for melt), then toppings.
Practical tip: Less is more — overload = soggy. Add delicate items (basil, prosciutto) after baking.
4. Baking
Temperature: 475–550°F (245–290°C) — the hotter the better for home ovens.
Timing:
With pizza stone/steel: 8–12 minutes (rotate halfway).
On regular baking sheet: 12–15 minutes.
Pizza is done when crust is golden-brown with some char spots and cheese is bubbling.

Best home hack: Preheat stone/sheet for 45–60 min at max temp. Launch pizza on parchment for first 5 min, then slide parchment out so bottom crisps directly on stone.

Practical Tips for Success
Crust issues: Too thick? Stretch thinner next time. Soggy bottom? Hotter oven + preheat longer + less sauce.
Cheese: Mix fresh mozzarella (flavor) with low-moisture shredded (less water release).
Oven variation: Gas vs electric matters — watch the first pizza closely and adjust.
Make-ahead: Dough and sauce keep 2–3 days in fridge. Freeze dough balls after first rise.
No stone? Use cast-iron skillet or grill (outdoor grill at high heat gives great results).
Scale toppings to your taste: Margherita (sauce, mozzarella, basil, olive oil drizzle after bake) is the easiest winner.

Start simple with Margherita for your first try — it teaches you the fundamentals fast. Once comfortable, experiment with toppings.

Enjoy your pizza night!
