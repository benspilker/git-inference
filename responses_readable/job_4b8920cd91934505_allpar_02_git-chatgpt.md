Using an ice cube to water an orchid is sometimes suggested for convenience, especially with Phalaenopsis orchids, but it’s not ideal. Here’s why:

Pros:

Slow melting can prevent overwatering.

Helps beginners avoid accidentally flooding the roots.

Cons:

Cold water can shock roots and slow growth.

Ice cubes may not distribute moisture evenly—roots might dry out in spots while others stay too wet.

Repeated cold exposure can stress the plant.

Better approach: Room-temperature water poured slowly over the potting medium until it drains out is safer and more effective for orchid health.
