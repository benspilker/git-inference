Homemade Pizza – Simple Step‑by‑Step Guide

1. Dough (makes 2 × 12‑inch pizzas)
Ingredient	Amount
Bread‑flour (high‑protein)	500 g (≈ 4 cups)
Water (lukewarm, ~38 °C)	320 ml (≈ 1 ⅓ cups)
Active‑dry yeast	5 g (1 tsp)
Fine sea salt	10 g (2 tsp)
Olive oil	15 ml (1 tbsp)
Optional: a pinch of sugar	2 g (½ tsp)

Method

Activate yeast – In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast (and sugar, if using) in the warm water. Let sit 5 min until foamy.
Mix dry – In a large bowl, whisk flour and salt together.
Combine – Make a well in the flour, pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead – Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 min (or 5 min in a stand‑mix with a dough hook on medium speed) until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
First rise – Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel. Let rise 1‑1½ h at room temperature, or until doubled in size.
Punch & divide – Deflate gently, split into 2 equal balls (or 3 for smaller pizzas). Shape each into a tight round, cover, and rest 15 min – this relaxes the gluten for easier stretching.
2. Sauce (quick & flavorful)
Ingredient	Amount
Canned whole‑peeled tomatoes (San Marzano)	400 g (1 can)
Extra‑virgin olive oil	15 ml (1 tbsp)
Fresh garlic, minced	2 cloves
Dried oregano	½ tsp
Dried basil	½ tsp
Pinch of sugar (optional)	–
Salt & pepper	to taste
Fresh basil leaves (optional)	a few, torn

Method

Blend – Crush the tomatoes by hand or pulse briefly in a food processor; keep a slightly chunky texture.
Season – Stir in olive oil, garlic, oregano, basil, sugar, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust. No need to cook; the sauce will finish in the oven, preserving bright tomato flavor.
3. Toppings (keep it simple)
Cheese: Fresh mozzarella (sliced or torn) – 150‑200 g per pizza.
Optional extras: Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, roasted red peppers, caramelized onions, fresh arugula, drizzle of olive oil, grated Parmesan.

Tip: If using watery mozzarella, pat dry with paper towels to avoid soggy crust.

4. Baking
Parameter	Recommendation
Oven type	Conventional or convection (no pizza stone required, but a stone or steel gives the best crust).
Pre‑heat temperature	250 °C (482 °F) – highest setting your oven allows (most home ovens max at 250 °C).
Pre‑heat time	At least 30 min with the stone/steel inside.
Baking surface	Pizza stone, steel, or upside‑down baking sheet.
Bake time	8‑12 min – watch for a golden‑brown crust and bubbling cheese.
Broiler	If you like extra char, switch to broiler for the last 1‑2 min (watch closely).

Procedure

Stretch dough – On a well‑floured surface, gently stretch each ball into a 12‑inch circle, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.
Add sauce – Spoon ¼‑⅓ cup of sauce, spreading thinly, leaving the rim untouched.
Cheese & toppings – Distribute mozzarella evenly, then add desired toppings.
Slide onto stone – Use a well‑floured pizza peel (or the back of a baking sheet) to transfer the pizza.
Bake – Slide onto the pre‑heated stone/steel. Bake 8‑12 min, rotating halfway for even browning.
Finish – Remove, drizzle a little olive oil, sprinkle fresh basil or Parmesan, and let rest 2 min before slicing.
5. Practical Tips for a Great Home Pizza
Tip	Why it helps
Use high‑protein flour (bread flour or “00” pizza flour)	Gives stronger gluten, yielding a chewy yet airy crust.
Cold‑ferment dough (24‑48 h in the fridge)	Develops deeper flavor and a crisper crust.
Pre‑heat stone/steel thoroughly	Mimics a brick oven’s instant heat, producing a blistered “leopard‑spot” crust.
Don’t overload sauce/toppings	Keeps the crust crisp; excess moisture makes it soggy.
Bake at the highest temperature	Fast bake creates a puffy edge and prevents drying out the toppings.
Use a pizza peel dusted with semolina	Prevents sticking and adds a subtle crunch.
Rotate the pizza	Ensures even browning, especially in ovens with hot spots.
Let the dough rest after shaping	Relaxes gluten, making it easier to stretch without tearing.
Finish with a drizzle of good olive oil or a sprinkle of flaky sea salt	Adds flavor depth and a pleasant mouthfeel.
