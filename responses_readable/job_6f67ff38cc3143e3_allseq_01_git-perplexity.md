Here’s a solid Neapolitan dough method you can use at home.

Ingredients

For about 4 pizza dough balls:

500 g Tipo 00 flour

325 g water

14 g salt

1 g instant yeast, or 2 g fresh yeast

This gives about 65% hydration, which is a common home-friendly range for a Neapolitan-style dough.

Step-by-step method

Mix the water and salt first.

Add the yeast and stir until dissolved.

Add about 10% of the flour and mix to make a slurry.

Add the rest of the flour gradually and mix until a rough dough forms.

Knead for 8–12 minutes, until smooth and elastic.

Cover and let the dough rest.

Divide into 4 equal balls, about 200–210 g each.

Ball the dough tightly and place in covered containers.

Let the dough ferment, then bring it back to room temperature before stretching.

Fermentation options
Same-day / warm room

Bulk ferment: 2–4 hours at room temperature.

Ball rest: 1–2 hours before baking.

Classic long room-temp ferment

Bulk ferment: 16–24 hours at room temperature.

Ball rest: 2–3 hours before baking.

Cold ferment

Bulk ferment: 1–2 hours at room temperature.

Refrigerate the dough balls for 24–72 hours.

Warm at room temperature for 2–3 hours before use.

Long fermentation improves flavor and extensibility, while cold fermentation is the easiest way to fit the process into a home schedule.

Stretching and baking

Use your fingertips to press the center outward, leaving a thicker rim.

Stretch gently by hand; do not use a rolling pin.

Top lightly so the dough stays supple.

Bake on a very hot stone or steel, as hot as your oven allows.

Common mistakes to avoid

Using too much yeast, which can make the dough taste yeasty and overproof quickly.

Over-kneading by machine, which can make the dough tough.

Skipping the final room-temperature proof, which makes stretching harder.

Baking in an oven that is not fully preheated.

Easy rule of thumb

If you want the most reliable home version, use 500 g flour, 325 g water, 14 g salt, and 1 g instant yeast, then cold ferment the dough balls for 24–48 hours before baking.
