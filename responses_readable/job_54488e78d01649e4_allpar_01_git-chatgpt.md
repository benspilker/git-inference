Here’s a clear, practical way to make a homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients

Dough (1 medium pizza):

2½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

2¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic (minced)

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings:

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Anything you like (pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, etc.)

Steps

1. Make the dough

Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Combine flour + salt in a bowl.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil, mix into dough.

Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours (until doubled).

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a pan.

Simmer 10–15 minutes, then set aside.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough to your preferred thickness.

Add sauce, cheese, and toppings.

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling.

5. Serve

Let cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.

Quick tips

Hot oven = better crust.

Don’t overload toppings (keeps it crisp).

A preheated baking sheet or pizza stone helps a lot.
