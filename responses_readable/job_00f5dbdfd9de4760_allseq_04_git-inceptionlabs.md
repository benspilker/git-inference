Here’s a straightforward, step‑by‑step recipe for a classic homemade pizza.

Ingredients
Dough (makes one medium 12‑inch pizza)
2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈ 110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil
Sauce
1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
Toppings (feel free to customize)
1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, bell peppers, olives, etc.
Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
Instructions
1. Prepare the dough
Activate yeast: In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let it sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt.
Combine: Pour the yeast mixture and olive oil into the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8–10 minutes, until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
First rise: Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise 1–2 hours, or until doubled in size.
2. Make the sauce
In a small saucepan, combine tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar (if using).
Simmer on low heat 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside to cool.
3. Preheat the oven
Preheat to 475 °F (245 °C).
If you have a pizza stone, place it on the middle rack while the oven heats.
4. Shape the crust
Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a lightly floured surface.
Roll or stretch it into a 12‑inch circle, about ¼‑inch thick.
Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pre‑heated pizza stone (use a pizza peel or the back of a baking sheet).
5. Assemble the pizza
Spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border for the crust.
Sprinkle mozzarella over the sauce.
Add your chosen toppings.
6. Bake
Slide the pizza into the oven.
Bake 12–15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
For extra browning, you can broil for 1–2 minutes at the end—watch closely!
7. Finish & serve
Remove the pizza from the oven and let it rest 2–3 minutes.
Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired.
Slice, serve, and enjoy!

Tips for a great crust

Hot oven: A fully pre‑heated oven (and stone, if using) gives a crisp bottom.
Don’t overload: Too many toppings can make the crust soggy.
Steam: A few drops of water in a pan on the lower rack create steam, helping the crust stay airy.
