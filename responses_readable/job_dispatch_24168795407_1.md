Pizza basics you can follow at home:

Dough: mix 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon sugar, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast. Add 3/4 cup warm water and 2 tablespoons olive oil; stir until a shaggy dough forms, then knead 8–10 minutes until smooth. Let rise in a warm spot until doubled (about 1–2 hours).

Sauce: simmer 1 cup crushed tomatoes with 1 clove minced garlic, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and a pinch of sugar for 10–15 minutes. Scrape from heat.

Assembly: preheat oven very hot (top options: 475–500°F / 245–260°C). Shape dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness, transfer to a baking sheet or pizza stone, spread sauce, sprinkle shredded mozzarella (and any toppings you like).

Bake: 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly. Optional: broil 1–2 minutes at the end for extra browning.

Tips: a hot oven and a pizza stone help achieve a crisp bottom crust; don’t overload with toppings; you can use 20-minute quick dough if you’re short on time.
