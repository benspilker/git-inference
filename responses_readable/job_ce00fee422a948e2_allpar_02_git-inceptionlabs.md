Ingredients
Category	Items
Dough	2 ¼ tsp active‑dry yeast (1 packet)
1 ½ cups warm water (≈ 110 °F/43 °C)
3 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
2 Tbsp olive oil
2 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
Sauce	1 cup canned crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp dried basil
Salt & pepper to taste
1 Tbsp olive oil
Toppings	2 cups shredded mozzarella
½ cup grated Parmesan (optional)
Fresh basil leaves
Other toppings of choice (pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, etc.)
Finishing	Olive oil for brushing crust
Red pepper flakes (optional)
Equipment
Large mixing bowl
Kitchen towel or plastic wrap
Baking sheet or pizza stone
Pizza peel or flat baking sheet (if using a stone)
Oven (pre‑heated to high temperature)
Step‑by‑Step

Activate the yeast

In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let sit 5‑10 minutes until foamy.

Make the dough

In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt.
Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 minutes (or use a stand‑mixer's dough hook on medium speed for ~5 minutes) until smooth and elastic.

First rise

Lightly oil the bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot 1‑1½ hours, or until doubled in size.

Prepare the sauce

While the dough rises, heat 1 Tbsp olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté 30 seconds.
Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer 10‑12 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside to cool.

Pre‑heat the oven

If using a pizza stone, place it on the middle rack and pre‑heat the oven to 475‑500 °F (245‑260 °C) for at least 30 minutes. A baking sheet works too; just pre‑heat the oven.

Shape the pizza

Punch down the risen dough and divide it (for two 12‑inch pizzas) or keep whole for one large pizza.
On a parchment‑lined sheet or a lightly floured surface, stretch or roll the dough into a round, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust. Aim for ¼‑inch thickness.

Add toppings

Spread a thin layer of sauce (≈ ¼‑½ cup) over the dough, leaving the edge clear.
Sprinkle mozzarella evenly, then add any additional toppings. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt.

Bake

Transfer the pizza (on parchment) to the hot stone or baking sheet.
Bake 10‑12 minutes, or until the crust is golden‑brown and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned.

Finish & serve

Remove the pizza, let it rest 2‑3 minutes. Add fresh basil leaves, a light drizzle of olive oil, and red pepper flakes if desired. Slice and enjoy!
Tips for Success
Hot oven = crisp crust. If you have a broiler, you can finish the pizza under it for 1‑2 minutes for extra char.
Steam: Placing a shallow pan of water on a lower rack helps create a crisp bottom while keeping the crust tender.
Dough storage: The dough can be refrigerated for up to 3 days (cover tightly) or frozen for longer storage. Thaw overnight in the fridge before shaping.
