Here's a practical, authentic Neapolitan pizza dough recipe based on traditional methods (aligned with AVPN guidelines: just flour, water, salt, and yeast; high hydration ~60%; long fermentation for flavor and airy texture). This makes about 4 dough balls (~250-280g each) for 10-12 inch pizzas.

Ingredients (for ~1 kg total dough)
Water: 600 ml (room temperature, ~20-22°C / 68-72°F; adjust slightly for flour absorption)
Tipo 00 pizza flour (e.g., Caputo Pizzeria or similar; W 250-320, protein ~11.5-13.5%): 1 kg (use high-quality Italian 00 for best extensibility and char)
Fine sea salt: 25-30 g (2.5-3%; dissolve fully in water)
Yeast: 0.1-2 g fresh brewer's yeast or ~0.03-0.7 g active dry/instant yeast (very little for long fermentation; scale based on temperature and desired timing—less in warmer conditions)

Baker's percentages (for scaling): 100% flour | 60% water | 2.5-3% salt | 0.01-0.2% dry yeast.

No oil, sugar, or other additives in true Neapolitan dough.

Step-by-Step Instructions
Dissolve salt: In a large bowl or mixer, add all the water and salt. Stir or mix by hand until salt fully dissolves (1-2 minutes). This prevents yeast damage later.
Add yeast and initial flour: Dissolve yeast in the salted water (crush fresh yeast between fingers if using). Add ~100-150 g flour (10-15%) and mix by hand or spoon until it forms a smooth, pancake-batter-like slurry. No lumps. Let rest 5-10 minutes.
Incorporate remaining flour: Gradually add the rest of the flour in stages while mixing. Use a fork/spiral mixer (or hand) for ~10-20 minutes total until the dough reaches the "dough point"—moist, soft, plastic, non-sticky, and pulls away cleanly from the bowl sides. It should feel slightly tacky but not wet. Avoid over-kneading (max ~20 min total; dough will heat up and oxidize).
Bulk fermentation (first rise): Form into a smooth ball, cover (damp cloth or plastic), and let rest at room temperature (~18-22°C / 64-72°F ideal). Rest 1-2 hours initially, or until it starts to show activity (slight increase in volume). For short same-day use: 8-12 hours total bulk.
Divide and ball: Gently turn out dough (don't punch down aggressively). Divide into 4 equal portions (~250-280 g each for standard Neapolitan size). Form tight balls using the "pinch-and-tuck" or "boule" method: fold edges under to create surface tension (smooth top, seams at bottom). Avoid tearing.
Final proofing/fermentation:
Room temperature option: Place balls in a lightly floured proofing box or covered tray. Proof 4-8 hours more (total fermentation 8-24 hours). Ready when balls are soft, airy, and pass the "poke test" (slow rebound).
Cold fermentation (preferred for flavor): After dividing, refrigerate balls (or bulk dough) in a sealed container for 24-48 hours (up to 72 for deeper taste). Dough continues slow fermentation. Remove 2-4 hours before use to reach room temp and finish proofing. Knock back gently if over-proofed.
Use the dough: Stretch by hand only (no rolling pin). Start from center, push outward, leaving a puffy cornicione (rim). Bake in a very hot oven (wood-fired ~450-500°C / 840-930°F for 60-90 seconds; home oven max temp with pizza steel/stone ~250-300°C / 480-570°F for 5-8 min).

Timing summary:

Mixing: 15-25 minutes.
Minimum total fermentation: 8-12 hours.
Optimal: 24+ hours (bulk + balls, room or cold).
Adjust yeast/temp: Warmer = less yeast/shorter time; cooler = more yeast or longer cold proof.
Fermentation Options
Same-day (8-12 hours): Higher yeast (1-2 g fresh), room temp throughout. Simpler but less complex flavor.
Overnight/room temp (12-24 hours): Minimal yeast; best balance.
Cold fridge (24-72 hours): Lowest yeast; develops tangy, nutty notes and better digestibility. Most forgiving for home schedules. Start bulk fermentation briefly at room temp before fridging.
Common Mistakes to Avoid
Wrong flour or low hydration: Use proper 00 (not all-purpose); too little water = tough/dense dough. Aim for 58-65% hydration—dough should be soft and extensible.
Too much yeast or short fermentation: Leads to bland, bready taste and poor rise. Long slow ferment = flavor and big airy holes.
Over-kneading: Causes tough, stringy, or oxidized dough. Stop when soft/plastic.
Under- or over-proofing: Under = dense/chewy; over = collapses, tears easily, or sour/flat. Use poke test and watch temp.
Aggressive handling: Don't punch down harshly or use rolling pin—ruins structure and gas. Stretch gently by hand.
Temperature issues: Cold water/ingredients slow things; hot mixing overheats dough. Keep consistent ~18-22°C.
Sticky dough when stretching: Usually under-proofed or too much bench flour. Let rest longer; use minimal semolina/flour on peel.
Scaling errors: Weigh ingredients precisely (volume measurements vary). Salt too low = weak gluten; direct salt-yeast contact >5 min kills yeast.

Practice a few batches—dough improves with experience. Scale recipe as needed and note your room temp/humidity for consistency. Enjoy the process; the long ferment makes the difference in that signature soft, leopard-spotted crust.
