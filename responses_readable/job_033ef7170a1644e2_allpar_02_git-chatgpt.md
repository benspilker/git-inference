Here’s a straightforward guide to making a classic homemade pizza:

Ingredients

For Dough (makes 1 medium pizza):

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

For Sauce:

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic, minced

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)

Toppings (suggested):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or any favorite toppings

Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

Prepare the Dough:

In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.

In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.

Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour mixture. Stir until dough forms.

Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise for 1–2 hours until doubled in size.

Make the Sauce:

Combine tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a small saucepan.

Simmer on low heat for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside.

Assemble the Pizza:

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.

Punch down the dough and roll it out to your desired thickness. Transfer to a baking sheet or preheated stone.

Spread sauce evenly over dough, leaving a small border.

Sprinkle cheese and add toppings.

Bake:

Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

Optional: Broil for 1–2 minutes at the end for extra browning.

Serve:

Let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing and enjoying!

💡 Tips:

Preheat the oven fully for a crispier crust.

Use a pizza stone for better bottom crust.

Don’t overload on toppings to avoid a soggy pizza.
