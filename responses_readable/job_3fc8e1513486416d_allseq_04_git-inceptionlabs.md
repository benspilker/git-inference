Here’s a concise, practical guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients (makes one medium pizza)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
2 ¼ tsp (1 packet) active‑dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈110 °F / 43 °C)
2 Tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings (adjust to taste)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, bell peppers, olives, etc.
Fresh basil (optional)

Steps

Activate yeast – In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5‑10 min until foamy.
Mix dough – In a large bowl combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil; stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Knead – Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 min until smooth and elastic.
First rise – Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel, and let rise 1‑2 hours (or until doubled).
Prepare sauce – While the dough rises, combine tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and optional sugar in a saucepan. Simmer on low 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
Preheat oven – Heat to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven now.
Shape dough – Punch down the risen dough, roll or stretch it to your desired thickness, and transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or preheated stone.
Assemble – Spread sauce evenly, leaving a small border. Sprinkle mozzarella and add your chosen toppings.
Bake – Cook 12‑15 min, until the crust is golden and the cheese bubbles and browns. For extra color, you can broil 1‑2 min at the end.
Finish – Let the pizza rest 2‑3 min, slice, and enjoy!

Tips for a great crust

Preheat the oven fully; a hot oven yields a crispier base.
Use a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet for better bottom heat.
Avoid overloading toppings to keep the crust from getting soggy.
