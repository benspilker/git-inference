Here’s a straightforward, reliable way to make a good homemade pizza from scratch:

1) Dough (about 2 medium pizzas)

Ingredients

2 ¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast

1 ½ cups warm water (not hot)

3 ½–4 cups all-purpose or bread flour

2 tbsp olive oil

1 tsp sugar

1 tsp salt

Steps

Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Add oil, salt, and 3½ cups flour. Mix until a dough forms.

Knead 8–10 min until smooth (add a bit more flour if sticky).

Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–1.5 hours until doubled.

2) Sauce (quick version)

1 cup crushed tomatoes

1–2 cloves garlic (minced)

1 tbsp olive oil

Salt + pepper

Optional: oregano or basil

Simmer everything for ~10–15 minutes to thicken slightly.

3) Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F / 245°C (very hot is key).

Roll or stretch dough into a circle.

Transfer to a baking sheet or pizza stone.

Spread a thin layer of sauce.

Add cheese (mozzarella works best).

Add toppings (pepperoni, veggies, etc.).

4) Bake

Bake 10–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

5) Finish

Let cool a couple minutes, slice, and eat.
