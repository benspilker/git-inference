Here’s a clear, step-by-step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients (for dough, sauce, and topping)

Dough (makes 1 medium pizza):

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic, minced

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)

Toppings (suggested):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or any favorite toppings

Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

Prepare the dough:

In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.

Add yeast mixture and olive oil to flour. Mix until a dough forms.

Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.

Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a towel, and let rise 1–2 hours, until doubled in size.

Make the sauce:

Combine tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a small saucepan.

Simmer on low heat for 10–15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.

Assemble the pizza:

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while preheating.

Punch down the dough and roll it out to your desired thickness. Transfer to a baking sheet or preheated stone.

Spread sauce evenly over dough, leaving a small border around the edges.

Sprinkle cheese and add toppings.

Bake:

Bake for 12–15 min, or until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Optional: Broil 1–2 min at the end for extra browning.

Serve:

Let the pizza cool for a few minutes, slice, and enjoy!

💡 Tips for a great pizza:

Preheat the oven thoroughly; a hot oven gives a crispier crust.

Use a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet for better bottom crust.

Less is more with toppings—overloading can make the pizza soggy.
