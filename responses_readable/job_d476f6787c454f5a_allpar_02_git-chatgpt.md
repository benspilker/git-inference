To keep an orchid healthy and blooming, follow these key tips:

1. Proper Lighting

Orchids need bright, indirect light. A south- or east-facing window is ideal.

Avoid direct sunlight as it can scorch the leaves.

If your orchid isn’t blooming, it may need more light. Consider using a grow light if natural light is insufficient.

2. Watering

Water orchids thoroughly but allow them to dry out between waterings. Overwatering is a common mistake.

Water once a week, or when the top inch of the potting mix feels dry. In the winter, reduce watering frequency.

Ensure your pot has good drainage to prevent waterlogged roots.

3. Humidity

Orchids thrive in humidity levels of 50-70%.

Use a humidity tray or a room humidifier if the air is dry. Misting the leaves lightly can also help.

Avoid letting water sit in the crown of the orchid (the center of the leaves), as this can lead to rot.

4. Temperature

Ideal temperature ranges: 60-75°F (16-24°C) during the day and slightly cooler at night.

Avoid placing your orchid near drafts, heating vents, or air conditioners.

5. Fertilizing

Fertilize orchids with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer (e.g., 30-10-10) designed for orchids.

Fertilize once a month during the growing season (spring to fall), and cut back in winter.

6. Repotting

Orchids should be repotted every 1-2 years to refresh the growing medium.

Use a well-draining orchid mix (e.g., bark or sphagnum moss).

Repot after blooming has finished.

7. Pruning and Bloom Care

After the blooms fade, you can prune the flower spike back to the base (for most orchids), or cut it just above a node to encourage a second bloom.

Be patient as orchids can take time to rebloom, sometimes months after a flowering cycle.

8. Rest Period

After blooming, orchids may enter a resting period where growth slows down. This is normal.

Reduce watering slightly, but continue providing appropriate light and temperature.
