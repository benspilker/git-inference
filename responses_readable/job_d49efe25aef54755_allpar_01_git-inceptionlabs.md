How to Position Yourself & Win Jobs

Create a niche‑focused profile

Use a clear headline (e.g., “AI Prompt Engineer – LLM & Diffusion Model Specialist”).
Add a short tagline that mentions your unique value (speed, ROI, industry experience).

Show concrete results

Include screenshots, before/after metrics, or short video demos.
Quantify impact (e.g., “Reduced manual data entry time by 80%”).

Offer tiered packages

Basic – low‑cost entry (e.g., 2 prompts, 1 automation).
Standard – core deliverable (e.g., 5 prompts, full workflow).
Premium – full service + revisions + post‑delivery support.

Leverage client‑centric proposals

Start with a brief “audit” or “discovery call” (free or low‑cost).
Outline steps: Discovery → Draft → Review → Final → Handoff.

Maintain fast communication

Set clear response‑time expectations (e.g., “I reply within 2 hours”).
Use video calls for complex briefings; record and share notes.

Collect and showcase reviews

Prompt satisfied clients to leave a review immediately after delivery.
Highlight 5‑star reviews that mention ROI or speed.

Stay up‑to‑date with tools

For AI work, keep a list of the latest LLMs (Gemini 1.5, Claude 3.5, etc.).
For no‑code, master new Zapier triggers or Make.com modules.
Quick “Getting Started” Checklist (choose one niche)
Pick a niche – e.g., “AI Prompt Engineering”.
Build a portfolio – create 3–5 sample projects with measurable outcomes.
Set up profile – headline, tagline, portfolio screenshots, tiered pricing.
Create a lead magnet – free 10‑prompt audit or 30‑minute consultation.
Launch a targeted ad – LinkedIn or Reddit (r/freelance, r/Entrepreneur).
Apply to 5 relevant job posts daily – tailor each proposal to the client’s brief.
Deliver fast & ask for feedback – aim for 48‑hour turnaround on first orders.
