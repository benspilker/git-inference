Here's a practical, step-by-step guide to make a classic homemade pizza:
Dough (for 2 medium pizzas)
500g bread flour (or all-purpose)
325ml warm water (~110°F/43°C)
7g instant yeast (1 packet)
10g salt
15ml olive oil
Optional: 5g sugar or honey to help yeast activate
Mix: Combine flour, yeast, salt (and sugar if using). Add water and olive oil. Mix until shaggy.
Knead: 8–10 minutes by hand (or 5–7 in a stand mixer) until smooth and elastic.
Rise: Place in oiled bowl, cover, let rise 1–2 hours at room temp until doubled. Or refrigerate 24–72h for better flavor.
Divide: Split into 2 balls, rest covered 15–30 min before shaping.
Assembly & Baking
Preheat: Oven to highest setting (500°F/260°C+). Use a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet if available.
Shape: Stretch dough on floured surface to 10–12 inch rounds. Don't roll—use hands to preserve air bubbles.
Top lightly:
2–3 tbsp tomato sauce (crushed San Marzano + salt + oregano)
Fresh mozzarella or low-moisture mozzarella, torn
Optional: basil, pepperoni, mushrooms, etc. (less is more)
Bake: 8–12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles. Rotate halfway if oven has hot spots.
Finish: Drizzle with olive oil, add fresh basil, slice immediately.
Pro Tips
Prevent soggy crust: Pre-bake dough 2–3 min before adding toppings, or use less sauce.
No stone? Use a preheated cast-iron skillet or upside-down baking sheet.
Flavor boost: Add a pinch of garlic powder to sauce, or finish with grated Parmesan.
