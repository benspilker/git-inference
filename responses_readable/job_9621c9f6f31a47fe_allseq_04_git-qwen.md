How to Make a Basic Pizza at Home
Dough (for 2 medium pizzas)
500g bread flour (or all-purpose)
325ml warm water (~110°F/43°C)
7g instant yeast (1 packet)
10g salt
15ml olive oil
Optional: 5g sugar for faster rise
Method: Mix dry ingredients, add water and oil. Knead 8–10 min until smooth. Let rise covered 1–2 hours (or refrigerate overnight for better flavor).
Sauce (simple & effective)
1 can (400g) crushed San Marzano tomatoes
2 cloves garlic, minced
15ml olive oil
Salt, pepper, dried oregano to taste
Optional: pinch of red pepper flakes
Method: Sauté garlic in oil 30 sec, add tomatoes and seasonings. Simmer 15–20 min. Blend if you prefer smooth sauce.
Assembly & Baking
Preheat oven to highest setting (500°F/260°C+). Use a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet if available.
Divide dough, rest 15 min. Stretch (don't roll) into 12-inch rounds.
Spread thin layer of sauce, leaving a ½-inch border.
Add toppings sparingly—less is more for crisp results.
Top with fresh mozzarella (torn) or low-moisture mozzarella (shredded).
Bake 8–12 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbles.
Finish with fresh basil, a drizzle of olive oil, or grated Parmesan.
Pro Tips
Pre-bake crust 2–3 min if using wet toppings (fresh veggies, pineapple).
Pat dry high-moisture ingredients to avoid soggy pizza.
Use semolina on your peel or tray to prevent sticking.
Let cheese come to room temp before baking for even melt.
