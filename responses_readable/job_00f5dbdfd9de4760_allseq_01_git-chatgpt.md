Here’s a clear, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients
Dough (1 medium pizza)

2½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic, minced

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings (your choice)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, peppers, olives, etc.

Fresh basil (optional)

Steps
1. Make the dough

Mix warm water + sugar + yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

In a bowl, combine flour + salt.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil. Mix into a dough.

Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a small pan.

Simmer 10–15 minutes on low. Set aside.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough to your desired thickness.

Place on baking sheet or pizza stone.

Spread sauce, add cheese, then toppings.

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Optional: broil 1–2 minutes for extra browning.

5. Serve

Let cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.

Practical tips

Preheat the oven well—heat = crispy crust.

Don’t overload toppings or it gets soggy.

A pizza stone or upside-down baking sheet improves texture.
