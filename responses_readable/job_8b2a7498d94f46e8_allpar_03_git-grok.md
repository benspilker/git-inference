Here's a straightforward way to make a basic homemade pizza (one 12-inch pie). Use store-bought dough if you want it faster.

Quick Dough (makes 2 crusts, ~1-2 hours total)
1⅓ cups (320ml) warm water (~110°F/43°C)
1 Tbsp sugar
2¼ tsp active dry or instant yeast
3¼ cups (about 410g) all-purpose or bread flour (plus extra for dusting)
2 Tbsp olive oil
2 tsp salt
Mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5-10 min until foamy.
Add flour, oil, and salt. Stir until a shaggy dough forms, then knead by hand (or mixer) 5-7 min until smooth and elastic.
Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 30-60 min (or overnight in fridge for better flavor) until doubled.
Punch down, divide in half, and let rest 10 min. Roll/stretch into a circle on a floured surface.
Simple No-Cook Sauce (enough for 2-3 pizzas)
1 (15 oz) can tomato sauce
1 (6 oz) can tomato paste
1-2 Tbsp dried oregano or Italian seasoning
1 tsp garlic powder
½ tsp salt
Pinch of sugar and black pepper (optional)

Mix everything in a bowl. Taste and adjust.

Assembly & Baking
Preheat oven as hot as it goes (475-550°F / 245-290°C). Use a pizza stone or baking sheet (preheat it 20-30 min if possible) sprinkled with cornmeal or flour.
Spread sauce thinly on dough, leaving a border.
Add toppings lightly: shredded mozzarella (or fresh), cheese first or after sauce depending on preference, then veggies/meats. Don't overload.
Bake 8-15 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbles (time varies by oven/temp/thickness).

Tips: Less is more on toppings to avoid sogginess. Brush crust edge with olive oil. Experiment with pepperoni, basil, veggies, or whatever you have.

Scale or simplify as needed—it's forgiving. Enjoy!
