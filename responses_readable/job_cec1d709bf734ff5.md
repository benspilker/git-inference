Direct answer:
Here’s a straightforward, practical way to make a pizza from scratch.

Gather basics:

Dough: flour (strong bread flour works well), water, yeast, salt, olive oil.

Sauce: crushed tomatoes or tomato passata, salt, a pinch of sugar, olive oil, optional garlic or oregano.

Toppings: shredded mozzarella, any extras you like (basil, mushrooms, pepperoni, olives, peppers).

Make the dough (about 2 large pizzas):

Activate yeast: whisk a teaspoon of sugar in about 250–300 ml lukewarm water, sprinkle in 1 packet (7 g) active dry yeast, and let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

Mix dough: in a large bowl, combine 500 g flour and 1½ tsp salt. Add the yeast mixture plus 1–2 tbsp olive oil. Stir to form a rough dough.

Knead: turn onto a floured surface and knead 8–12 minutes until smooth and elastic.

Rise: shape into a ball, place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm spot until doubled (about 1–2 hours).

Divide: knock back the dough, divide into 2 portions for large pizzas, or 4 for smaller crusts. Shape into tight balls and let rest 15–20 minutes.

Make the sauce (while dough rises):

Simmer 400 g crushed tomatoes with 1–2 cloves minced garlic, a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar, and a drizzle of olive oil for 15–20 minutes. Taste and adjust.

Pre-heat and shape:

Heat oven as hot as it will go (ideally 250–280°C / 480–540°F) and preheat a baking sheet or a pizza stone if you have one.

Roll or stretch dough: on a floured surface, stretch each dough ball into a circle about 25–30 cm (9–12 inches) across. Leave a thicker edge for the crust.

Assemble:

Spoon a thin layer of sauce, leaving a border. Add shredded mozzarella and your chosen toppings.

Bake:

Transfer to the hot sheet/stone. Bake 8–12 minutes, until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbling. For a crisper base, bake directly on a hot stone if available.

Finish and serve:

Remove from oven, drizzle with a little olive oil or fresh basil, slice, and enjoy.

Tips to tailor:

For a chewier crust, let dough rise slowly in the fridge overnight (2nd rise after shaping, 1–2 hours at room temp before baking).

For a thinner crust, roll the dough very thin and bake at the highest heat possible.

If you don’t have a pizza stone, a heavy steel pan or inverted baking sheet works well.
