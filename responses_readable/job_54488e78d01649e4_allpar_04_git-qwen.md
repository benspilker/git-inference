Here's a clear, step-by-step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch:
Ingredients
Dough (makes 1 medium pizza):
2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)
2 tbsp olive oil
Sauce:
1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)
Toppings (suggested):
1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or your favorites
Fresh basil for garnish (optional)
Instructions
Prepare the dough:
Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water; let sit 5–10 min until foamy.
Mix flour and salt in a large bowl.
Add yeast mixture and olive oil; mix until dough forms.
Knead on a floured surface 8–10 min until smooth and elastic.
Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.
Make the sauce:
Combine tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a saucepan.
Simmer on low 10–15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
Assemble:
Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). Place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside to preheat.
Punch down dough and roll to desired thickness. Transfer to baking sheet or stone.
Spread sauce evenly, leaving a small border.
Add cheese and toppings.
Bake:
Bake 12–15 min until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
Optional: Broil 1–2 min at the end for extra browning.
Serve:
Let cool 2–3 minutes, slice, and enjoy!
💡 Pro tips:
Preheat thoroughly—a hot oven = crispier crust.
Use a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet for better bottom heat.
