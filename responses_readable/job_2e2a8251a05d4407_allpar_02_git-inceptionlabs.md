Here’s a clear, step‑by‑step guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients (makes 1 medium pizza)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
1 cup warm water (~110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Toppings (suggested)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or any favorite toppings
Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
1. Prepare the dough
In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit 5‑10 min until foamy.
In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.
Add the yeast mixture and olive oil to the flour. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8‑10 min, until smooth and elastic.
Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a towel, and let rise 1‑2 hours, or until doubled in size.
2. Make the sauce
Combine tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and optional sugar in a small saucepan.
Simmer on low heat 10‑15 min, stirring occasionally. Set aside to cool.
3. Assemble the pizza
Preheat your oven to 475 °F (245 °C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats.
Punch down the risen dough and roll it out to your desired thickness (about 12‑14 inches). Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a preheated stone.
Spread the sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
Sprinkle mozzarella and add your chosen toppings.
4. Bake
Bake for 12‑15 min, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
For extra browning, you can broil for 1‑2 min at the end (watch closely).
5. Serve
Let the pizza rest a few minutes, slice, and enjoy!

Tips for a great pizza

Hot oven → crispier crust.
Pizza stone or inverted baking sheet → better bottom crust.
Don’t overload toppings → prevents soggy crust.

Enjoy your homemade pizza!
