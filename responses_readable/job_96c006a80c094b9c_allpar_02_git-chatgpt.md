Tie-dye as a technique dates back thousands of years. The earliest known examples come from Asia and Africa:

In China, resist-dyeing techniques similar to tie-dye were used as early as 500–300 BCE.

In India, bandhani (a tie-dye style using small tied knots) has been practiced for over 2,000 years.

In Africa, indigo-dyeing with tied resist patterns has been documented for centuries, especially in Mali and Nigeria.

The colorful, spiral-and-polka-dot style popular in the West became widely known in the 1960s United States during the hippie movement.
