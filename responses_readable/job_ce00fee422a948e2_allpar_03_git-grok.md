Here's a simple, classic homemade pizza recipe for one large pizza (serves 2-4). Use store-bought dough or make a quick no-knead version if you want.

Ingredients

Dough (or buy fresh dough):

2¼ tsp active dry yeast
1 tsp sugar
1 cup warm water (105-110°F)
2½ cups all-purpose flour
2 tbsp olive oil
1 tsp salt

Sauce:

1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes
2 garlic cloves, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

Toppings (customize):

8-10 oz shredded mozzarella
Fresh basil, pepperoni, veggies, sausage, etc.
Extra olive oil for drizzling
Steps
Dough: Dissolve yeast + sugar in warm water. Let sit 5 min until foamy. Mix in flour, oil, salt. Knead 5-7 min until smooth. Cover and let rise 1 hour (or use immediately for thin crust).
Sauce: Simmer crushed tomatoes, garlic, oregano, salt, and flakes for 10-15 min. Cool slightly.
Assemble:
Preheat oven to 475-500°F (as hot as it goes). Place pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside to heat.
Stretch dough into 12-14" circle on floured surface/parchment.
Spread thin layer of sauce, leaving ½" border.
Add cheese and toppings sparingly (less is more for crispiness).
Bake: Slide onto hot stone/sheet. Bake 10-15 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbles. Rotate halfway if needed.
Finish: Rest 2 min, slice, add fresh basil if using.

Tips:

Hot oven = better crust.
Don't overload toppings or it gets soggy.
For crispier bottom, use cornmeal under dough.

Enjoy! Customize freely.
