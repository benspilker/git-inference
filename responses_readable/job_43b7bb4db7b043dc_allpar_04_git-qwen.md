Homemade Pizza: Complete Practical Guide
🍕 Dough (makes 3 medium ~11" pizzas)
Ingredients 
www.acouplecooks.com
:
500g Tipo 00 flour (or bread flour/all-purpose)
338g warm water (67.6% hydration—ideal balance for stretch + airy crumb)
8g instant yeast (2 tsp)
7g kosher salt (1 tsp)
13g olive oil (1 tbsp)
Method:
Mix flour, yeast, salt; add water + oil. Stir to shaggy dough.
Knead 8 min (stand mixer or by hand) until smooth, elastic, springs back when poked.
Divide into 3 balls, brush with oil, cover, rise 45–60 min at room temp until doubled.
Optional pro move: Refrigerate dough balls 2–3 days for deeper, nutty flavor

🍅 Sauce (no-cook, 5 minutes)

1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
3 garlic cloves, minced
1 tsp balsamic vinegar
½ tsp dried oregano
½ tsp fine salt
Optional: up to 1 tsp sugar if tomatoes taste acidic
Method: Whisk tomato paste with a splash of crushed tomatoes to loosen, then add remaining ingredients. Taste and adjust. Keeps 5 days refrigerated.

💡 Practical Tips
Stretching: Let dough rest 1 min if it resists stretching; gravity + knuckles work better than rolling pin 
www.acouplecooks.com
.
Hydration note: 67% hydration gives airy crumb while staying manageable. If too sticky, dust lightly with flour—don't over-flour 
www.acouplecooks.com
.
Toppings: Less is more. Sauce thinly (⅓–½ cup per pizza), cheese sparingly to avoid sogginess.
Transfer: Build pizza on a floured peel (or parchment) for easy slide onto hot stone.
Finish: Let pizza rest 1–2 min after baking before slicing to set cheese.
Pro shortcut: Make dough ahead and freeze individual balls. Thaw overnight in fridge, then bring to room temp 30–45 min before stretching
