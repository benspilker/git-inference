Here's a simple, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza (like Margherita) from scratch. This yields one 12-inch pizza; scale as needed.

1. Quick Pizza Dough (makes 2 balls, ~10-15 min active + 1-2 hr rise)
Ingredients (for one batch):
¾ cup (180 ml) warm water (100-110°F / ~38°C)
1 tsp active dry yeast
1 tsp sugar
2 cups (240g) all-purpose or bread flour (plus extra for dusting)
1 tbsp olive oil
¾ tsp salt
Steps:
In a bowl or stand mixer, stir warm water + yeast + sugar. Let sit 5-7 minutes until foamy.
Add flour, oil, and salt. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then knead 5 minutes (by hand or mixer) until smooth and elastic. Add a sprinkle of flour if too sticky.
Oil a bowl, place dough inside, cover, and let rise in a warm spot until doubled (1-2 hours, or overnight in fridge for better flavor).

Tip: For no-rise version, some recipes skip proofing and use the dough right away, but rising gives better texture.

2. Simple Sauce (makes enough for 2 pizzas)
Crush or blend 1 can (14 oz / 400g) San Marzano or good-quality tomatoes.
Mix with ½ tsp salt, 1 minced garlic clove, pinch of sugar, and optional oregano or basil. No cooking needed for fresh taste.
3. Assemble & Bake
Preheat oven as hot as it goes (ideally 500-550°F / 260-290°C) with a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside for at least 30 minutes.
Punch down risen dough, divide in half. On floured surface, stretch/roll one ball into a thin 12-inch circle (don't overwork it).
Transfer to parchment or cornmeal-dusted peel/pan.
Spread a thin layer of sauce (not too much—leave a border for crust).
Top sparingly: torn fresh mozzarella (7-8 oz / 200g), a few fresh basil leaves (add most after baking), drizzle of olive oil, pinch of salt.
Slide onto hot stone/sheet. Bake 8-12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly and spotted.
4. Finish
Remove, add fresh basil, optional red pepper flakes or grated Parmesan. Let cool 1-2 minutes before slicing.

Variations: Add pepperoni, veggies, or ham—keep toppings light to avoid soggy crust.
Pro tips: Hot oven + thin crust = best results at home. Use a pizza peel for easy transfer. Dough can be frozen after rising.

This is beginner-friendly and customizable. If you want a specific style (e.g., thick crust, no yeast, or vegan), let me know!
