Here’s a simple way to make pizza at home.

Ingredients

For 1 medium pizza:

2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour.

1 tsp salt.

1 tsp sugar.

1 packet active dry yeast.

1 cup warm water.

2 tbsp olive oil.

For the sauce:

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes.

1 clove garlic, minced.

1 tsp dried oregano.

1/2 tsp salt.

1/2 tsp sugar, optional.

Toppings:

Mozzarella cheese.

Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or anything you like.

Steps

Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.

In a bowl, combine flour and salt.

Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.

Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8 to 10 minutes until smooth.

Put it in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it, and let it rise for 1 to 2 hours.

Mix the sauce ingredients in a small pan and simmer for 10 to 15 minutes.

Heat your oven to 475 F. If you have a pizza stone, preheat it too.

Roll out the dough, add sauce, cheese, and toppings.

Bake for 12 to 15 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

Let it cool for a few minutes, then slice and eat.

Faster version

If you want it quicker, you can use a no-rise dough recipe that takes about 20 to 30 minutes total and skips the long wait for rising. One quick version uses yeast, flour, warm water, olive oil, and a short rest before baking.

Small tips

Don’t overload the pizza with toppings or it can turn soggy.

A hot oven helps the crust get crisp.

A pizza stone or baking sheet works well for better bottom browning.
