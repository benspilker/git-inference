Here’s a clear, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients

Dough (1 medium pizza)

2½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic (minced)

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

Optional: ½ tsp sugar

Toppings

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, veggies, mushrooms, etc.

Optional: fresh basil

Steps

1. Make the dough

Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Combine flour + salt in a bowl. Add yeast mixture + olive oil.

Mix into dough, then knead 8–10 min until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a pan.

Simmer 10–15 min on low, then set aside.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough. Place on baking sheet or pizza stone.

Spread sauce, add cheese and toppings.

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 min until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Optional: broil 1–2 min for extra browning.

5. Serve

Let cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.

Quick tips

Hot oven = crispier crust

Don’t overload toppings

Preheat your baking surface if possible
