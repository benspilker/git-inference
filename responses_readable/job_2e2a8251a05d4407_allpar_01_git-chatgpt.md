Here’s a clear, practical way to make a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients

Dough (1 medium pizza):

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic, minced

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional)

Toppings (example):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, veggies, mushrooms, olives, etc.

Fresh basil (optional)

Steps
1. Make the dough

Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.

Combine flour and salt in a bowl.

Add yeast mixture + olive oil. Mix into a dough.

Knead 8–10 minutes until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.

2. Make the sauce

Combine all sauce ingredients in a pan.

Simmer on low for 10–15 minutes.

3. Assemble

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll out dough.

Add sauce, cheese, and toppings (don’t overload).

4. Bake

Bake 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles.

Optional: broil 1–2 minutes for extra browning.

5. Serve

Let cool a few minutes, slice, and eat.

Practical tips

Hot oven = better crust (don’t skip preheating).

Use a preheated baking sheet or pizza stone if possible.

Keep toppings light to avoid soggy pizza.
