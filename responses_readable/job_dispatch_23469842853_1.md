Indiana has plenty of affordable and family-friendly spots that would be great for a day trip with a 12-month-old! Here are a few ideas that are fun, easy on the budget, and suitable for little ones:

1. Indianapolis Zoo & White River Gardens (Indianapolis)

Why it's great: The Indianapolis Zoo has lots of animal exhibits, and the White River Gardens are beautiful for a stroll. Your little one will enjoy seeing the animals and the sensory experience of the flowers and greenery.

Cost: General admission is relatively affordable, and there are discounts for younger children (under 2 are free).

Tip: The zoo has stroller-friendly paths, and there’s a good mix of indoor and outdoor spaces.

2. Turkey Run State Park (Marshall)

Why it's great: If you’re looking for a more nature-focused day, Turkey Run offers easy hiking trails, scenic views, and a historic covered bridge. While it’s great for adults, a 12-month-old will enjoy being out in nature, especially with some peaceful moments by the creek or on the short, easy trails.

Cost: A small entrance fee to the state park.

Tip: Pack a picnic! The park has nice areas for lunch, and it’s a perfect spot to relax with your little one.

3. Eagle Creek Park (Indianapolis)

Why it's great: One of the largest city parks in the U.S., Eagle Creek Park offers scenic walking trails, lakes, and picnic areas. It’s a peaceful place to spend the day with a little one, and there are plenty of stroller-friendly paths.

Cost: There’s a small fee for parking.

Tip: Take a slow stroll along the lake or check out the playground, which might be great if your little one is starting to explore more.

4. The Children's Museum of Indianapolis (Indianapolis)

Why it's great: While this museum is known for its interactive exhibits aimed at older kids, there’s still a lot to engage a toddler. The museum has sensory-friendly areas and activities, including a fantastic play area for younger children.

Cost: Admission is a bit higher, but they often have discounts or free days for residents. Under 2 is free.

Tip: Check out the "Pillars of the Earth" exhibit or the "Dinosaur Exhibit" — both offer safe and engaging experiences for little ones.

5. Cataract Falls (Cloverdale)

Why it's great: If you like waterfalls, Cataract Falls is Indiana’s largest waterfall, and it’s a beautiful spot for a picnic or a peaceful stroll. The falls have a viewing platform that’s easy to access.

Cost: Free.

Tip: It's a very short walk to the falls, making it ideal for a family outing with a 12-month-old.

6. Brown County State Park (Nashville)

Why it's great: Known for its rolling hills and beautiful landscapes, Brown County is a lovely place to get out and enjoy nature. The park has some easy trails, and you can enjoy a nice drive around the park, which is great if your baby naps during car rides.

Cost: A small entrance fee to the park.

Tip: If your little one enjoys the outdoors, the park has plenty of wide open spaces to crawl around or explore.

7. Conner Prairie (Fishers)

Why it's great: This historical park has some fun outdoor areas where you can explore with a toddler. There are farm animals, beautiful walking paths, and a very peaceful setting. While it's historically focused, it has plenty of interactive areas.

Cost: There’s an admission fee, but it’s affordable.

Tip: Spend time in the "Animal Encounters" area or explore the beautiful grounds and let your little one enjoy nature.

8. Indiana Dunes State Park (Chesterton)

Why it's great: If you’re near the northern part of Indiana, this is a great place to visit. The beach area is calm and stroller-friendly, and the dunes are a lot of fun to explore.

Cost: Small entrance fee.

Tip: You could dip your toes in the lake or enjoy a quiet moment by the water while your baby takes in the sights and sounds.

9. Monon Trail (Indianapolis)

Why it's great: This is a well-maintained trail that runs from Indianapolis into suburban areas. You can take a relaxing walk or bike ride with your baby in a stroller. There are several parks and green spaces along the way.

Cost: Free to use the trail.

Tip: Plan to stop at one of the parks along the trail for a picnic or a play break.

10. Indiana State Museum (Indianapolis)

Why it's great: While this museum is often geared toward older kids and adults, there are tactile and sensory exhibits that can entertain a younger child. The museum is full of interesting things to look at, and there’s a nice cafe if you need to take a break.

Cost: Affordable admission, and under 2 are free.

Tip: There are quiet spots for feeding and relaxing if needed.

All these places offer easy access, nature, and a relaxing atmosphere for parents and babies alike. Just make sure to bring a diaper bag, snacks, and a stroller for your little one.
