Here's a simple, practical way to make homemade pizza from scratch (dough + sauce + toppings). This yields one large or two smaller pizzas and takes about 1–2 hours total, mostly hands-off rising time.

1. Quick Pizza Dough (makes 2 crusts)

Ingredients (basic version):

¾ cup (180 ml) warm water (105–110°F / 40–43°C)
1 tsp active dry yeast (or instant)
1 tsp sugar
2 cups (250–280 g) all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
1 tsp salt
1–2 Tbsp olive oil

Steps:

Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar in a bowl. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
Add flour, salt, and olive oil. Stir until a shaggy dough forms, then knead by hand (or mixer) for 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Add a sprinkle of flour if too sticky.
Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm spot for 30–60 minutes (or up to overnight in fridge for better flavor). Dough should double in size.⁠Sallysbakingaddiction

No-rise shortcut: Skip the rise and use immediately (still works fine for a thinner crust).

2. Simple Pizza Sauce
1 can (14–15 oz) crushed or diced tomatoes (or fresh tomatoes, blended)
1–2 garlic cloves, minced
1 tsp dried oregano (or Italian seasoning)
½ tsp salt, pinch of sugar, black pepper
Optional: fresh basil, olive oil, or a splash of tomato paste for thickness

Mix everything (no cooking needed for fresh taste). Makes enough for 2 pizzas. Spread thinly.

3. Assemble & Bake

Toppings ideas (keep it light to avoid sogginess):

Classic Margherita: sauce, fresh mozzarella slices, fresh basil (add after baking)
Pepperoni: sauce, shredded mozzarella, lots of pepperoni
Veggie: sauce, mozzarella, sliced bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, olives
Other favorites: sausage, ham + pineapple, BBQ chicken, or pesto base instead of tomato sauce

Steps:

Preheat oven as hot as it goes (475–550°F / 245–290°C). Use a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet if possible—preheat it for 20–30 minutes for crispier crust.
Divide dough in half. On a floured surface, stretch or roll into a 12-inch circle (don't worry about perfect shape).
Transfer to parchment paper or cornmeal-dusted peel/pan. Spread thin layer of sauce, add cheese, then toppings.
Bake 8–12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese bubbles. Rotate halfway if needed. Let cool 2 minutes before slicing.

Tips for better results:

Less is more on toppings—too many make it soggy.
Brush crust edges with olive oil for golden color.
For extra crisp: bake on lowest oven rack or use a pizza steel.
Store-bought dough works in a pinch if you're short on time.

Enjoy fresh out of the oven—way better than delivery. Experiment with toppings once you nail the basics! If you want a specific style (e.g., deep dish or no-yeast), let me know.
