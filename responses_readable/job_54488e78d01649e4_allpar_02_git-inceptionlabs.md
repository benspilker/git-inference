Here’s a straightforward, practical guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch.

Ingredients (for one medium pizza)

Dough

2 ½ cups all‑purpose flour
1 tsp salt
1 tsp sugar
1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active‑dry yeast
1 cup warm water (≈ 110 °F / 43 °C)
2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
1 clove garlic, minced
1 tsp dried oregano
½ tsp salt
½ tsp sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Toppings (feel free to customize)

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella
Pepperoni, sliced mushrooms, bell peppers, olives, etc.
Fresh basil leaves (optional)
Step‑by‑Step Instructions

Activate the yeast

In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water.
Let sit 5‑10 minutes until foamy.

Mix the dough

In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt.
Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.

Knead

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface.
Knead 8‑10 minutes, until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.

First rise

Lightly oil a clean bowl, place the dough inside, cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap.
Let rise 1‑2 hours, or until doubled in size.

Prepare the sauce

While the dough rises, combine tomato sauce, minced garlic, oregano, salt, and sugar in a small saucepan.
Simmer on low 10‑15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside.

Preheat the oven

Preheat to 475 °F (245 °C).
If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven now to heat up.

Shape the crust

Punch down the risen dough.
On a floured surface, roll or stretch it to a 12‑inch circle (or your desired shape).
Transfer to a parchment‑lined baking sheet or a pre‑heated pizza stone.

Assemble

Spread a thin layer of sauce, leaving a small border for the crust.
Sprinkle mozzarella evenly.
Add your chosen toppings.

Bake

Slide the pizza into the hot oven.
Bake 12‑15 minutes, until the crust is golden and the cheese bubbles and lightly browns.
For extra browning, you can broil for 1‑2 minutes at the end (watch closely).

Finish & serve

Remove from oven, let rest 2‑3 minutes.
Add fresh basil or a drizzle of olive oil if desired.
Slice, serve, and enjoy!
Quick Tips for a Better Pizza
Hot oven = crispier crust.
Pizza stone (or an inverted baking sheet) gives a better bottom crust.
Don’t overload toppings; they can make the pizza soggy.
Thin dough cooks faster and stays crispier, while a thicker dough yields a chewier bite.
