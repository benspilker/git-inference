Here’s a concise, practical guide to making a classic homemade pizza from scratch:

Ingredients (1 medium pizza):
Dough:

2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

1 tsp salt

1 tsp sugar

1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast

1 cup warm water (~110°F / 43°C)

2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce:

1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes

1 clove garlic, minced

1 tsp dried oregano

½ tsp salt

½ tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)

Toppings (suggested):

1–2 cups shredded mozzarella

Pepperoni, vegetables, olives, mushrooms, or favorites

Fresh basil (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare dough:

Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water; sit 5–10 min until foamy.

Mix flour and salt; add yeast mixture + olive oil. Knead 8–10 min until smooth.

Cover and let rise 1–2 hours until doubled.

Make sauce:

Simmer tomato sauce, garlic, oregano, salt, sugar for 10–15 min.

Assemble pizza:

Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).

Roll dough, transfer to baking sheet/stone. Spread sauce, add cheese + toppings.

Bake:

12–15 min until crust is golden and cheese bubbly. Optional: broil 1–2 min for extra browning.

Serve:

Let cool a few minutes, slice, enjoy.

💡 Tips:

Preheat oven thoroughly for crisp crust.

Less is more with toppings to avoid soggy pizza.

Using a pizza stone or inverted sheet improves the bottom crust.
