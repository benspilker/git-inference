Pizza making, quick version:

Use a no-rise dough method: mix 2 1/4 to 2 1/2 cups flour, 1 packet active dry yeast, 1 teaspoon sugar, 1 teaspoon salt, 2 tablespoons olive oil, and 1 cup warm water. Knead briefly into a cohesive ball, then roll out to 12–14 inches. No long rise needed.

Sauce: 1 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, 1 clove garlic (minced), oregano, salt to taste. Simmer 5–10 minutes if you have time.

Toppings: mozzarella, plus any favorites (pepperoni, veg, olives). Don’t overload.

Bake: preheat oven hot (450–500°F / 230–260°C) with a pizza stone or heavy baking sheet inside. Bake 10–12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.

Optional quick dough variations:

10-minute crust (no rise) using a food processor or mixer to blend dry ingredients with warm water and oil; rest briefly (5–20 minutes) while toppings are prepped.

No-knead version: mix ingredients until just combined, flatten into a disk, top, and bake on a preheated pan; this can yield a lighter crust with less handling.
